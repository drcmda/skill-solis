# 05 — Maintenance

> **Source:** Solis 26 Operator Manual (International Tractors Ltd / Sonalika), Publication No. S2618T/J20/28, Rev.1 Aug 2020 — model: Solis 26, Tractor with 9+9 Speed Transmission, Part Code 300190111A — Chapter 05, pp.67–82.

This chapter is the complete maintenance reference for the Solis 26 tractor: the periodic maintenance schedule, fuel/oil/coolant servicing, filter and air-cleaner care, brake and clutch free-play adjustment, axle and transmission oil changes, recommended lubricants, electrical-system care (battery, alternator, starter, fuses), greasing and lifting points, and long-idle storage precautions. All capacities, intervals, torque/tension values, and figure callouts are reproduced.

> **IMPORTANT (general note for entire schedule):** This maintenance schedule applies to tractors operated under normal conditions. When the tractor is frequently operated in muddy places, greasing must be carried out more frequently; when often operated in dusty places, clean the air cleaner element and fuel filter more frequently. Extra servicing must be carried out according to the particular situation.

## 5.2 Fuel

**Fuel Tank Capacity: 29 ±3 Liters (7.66 ±0.79 US Gallons).**

### 5.2.1 Fuel Tank Filling

> **CAUTION:** Comply with the following instructions when working with diesel fuel:
> 1. Do not smoke while filling the fuel tank because diesel is a flammable liquid and catches fire easily.
> 2. Mixtures of diesel fuel and alcohol are not approved since the resulting lubrication of the fuel injection system is insufficient.
> 3. Clean the area around the filler neck where the fuel is poured.
> 4. Fill the tank at the end of the day to prevent the formation of overnight condensation.
> 5. Never remove the plug or fuel the tractor while the engine is running. Keep control of the pump nozzle whilst the tank is being filled.
> 6. The tank must not be completely filled. Allow space for an increase in volume. If the original tank plug is lost, it must be replaced with an original spare which must be fully tightened.
> 7. Dry up any fuel spill immediately.

**Fig. 5.2b — Fuel Tank Cap:** Photo showing the fuel tank cap (filler plug) located on top of the tank; tighten the plug properly after fuelling.

### 5.2.2 Fuel Requisites

It is important to use good quality fuel for the long life and good performance of the engine. Fuels must be clean, well refined and non-corrosive for the fuel system components. Make sure you use fuel of a known quality and reliable origin.

### 5.2.3 Fueling

Before fueling the tractor, clean the zone around the filler neck to prevent foreign bodies from entering the tank. After fuelling, tighten the plug properly.

### 5.2.4 Fuel Storage

Take all the necessary precautions to ensure that stored fuel does not become polluted with dirt, water or other substances.

- Store fuel in **black iron cans. Do not store it in galvanized cans** as the galvanization treatment would react with the fuel and form compounds that would spoil the injection pump and injectors.
- Plan fuel purchases so that summer fuels are not kept too long and used in winter.
- To make sludge and water condensation easier to remove, there should be a discharge plug **(c)** at the lowest point, on the opposite side to the drain tube.
- If the fuel is not filtered from the storage can, use a funnel with a fine gauge mesh over the fuel tank fill plug inlet when fueling.
- Store fuel cans away from direct sunlight and slightly tilted, so that any sediment inside is eliminated through the outlet tube.

|---------|------|

## 5.3 Engine Oil

**Recommended grade of Engine Oil:** SAE-10W40 (cold countries), SAE-15W40 (hot countries).

### 5.3.1 Checking Engine Oil Level

Check the oil level by unscrewing the dipstick (located at the RHS of the engine). Top up if the level is below the minimum mark. Do not overfill past the maximum mark. Oil level should be between the maximum and minimum marks (see Fig. 5.3a).

Before checking the oil level, ensure the tractor is parked on level ground. Stop the engine and wait some time so all oil returns to the oil sump.

**Fig. 5.3a — Dipstick marks:** Dipstick showing upper MAX line and lower MIN line; oil level must lie between them.

### 5.3.2 Replacement of Oil Filter & Engine Oil

**Changing Oil Filter:**

1. Remove the oil filter by rotating it anti-clockwise by hand or with a filter wrench.
2. Take the new oil filter and check it for proper seating of the gasket.
3. Apply clean engine oil to the gasket on the new oil filter.
4. Install the oil filter. When the filter gasket contacts the mounting surface of the filter, tighten the new oil filter.

**Refilling Engine Oil:**

1. Re-install the drain plug and remove the oil filler cap.
2. Fill with the specified engine oil to the specified sump-capacity level from the oil filler cap (Fig. 5.3c & 5.3d).
3. Insert the oil level gauge (dipstick) into its guide, then pull it out again.
4. Ensure the oil level is between the MAX. & MIN. marks on the dipstick. If less, pour oil to bring it to the specified level.
5. Install the oil filler cap after a refill.
6. Check the oil pan and other parts for oil leakage.
7. Start the engine, allow it to run idle and don't race it immediately.

**Figures:** Fig. 5.3b shows the **Engine Oil Filter** location (canister filter on engine). Fig. 5.3c and Fig. 5.3d show the **Oil Filler Cap** location on the engine.

## 5.4 Replacement of Fuel Filter

1. Turn off the ignition switch.
2. Remove the filter by rotating it anti-clockwise by hand or with a special wrench.
3. Take the new filter and check it for proper seating of the gasket.
4. Apply clean engine oil to the gasket on the new fuel filter.
5. Install the fuel filter; when the filter gasket contacts the mounting surface of the filter, tighten the filter and ensure there is no leakage.

**Fig. 5.4a — Fuel Filter:** Photo showing the spin-on fuel filter element. After changing the fuel filter the system must be air-bled (see §5.5).

---

## 5.5 Air Bleeding of Fuel System

After changing the fuel filter the system must be air-bled as follows:

1. Loosen the vent plug **(A)** at the top of the fuel filter body.
2. Loosen the vent plug **(B)** of the FIP (fuel injection pump) and allow air to flow out of the system.
3. Switch ON the ignition key to start the electric pump until completion of the air bleeding process.
4. Tighten the vent plug **(B)** until bubble-free fuel flows from the return valve.
5. Tighten the vent plug **(A)** until bubble-free fuel flows from the air vent plug hole.

**Fig. 5.4b — Bleed points:** Photo identifying vent plug **(A)** at top of fuel filter and vent plug **(B)** on the FIP.

## 5.6 Radiator

Coolant is a mixture of water and an anti-rusting / anti-freezing agent in a recommended ratio.

### 5.6.1 Coolant Level in Radiator (When Hot)

> **WARNING / procedure (hot system):** Slowly open the radiator cap (1, Fig. 5.6a) up to the safety catch (about 1/3 turn). Wait to allow the steam to escape. Continue opening the cap, press it down firmly to release the safety catch. The level of coolant should just touch the tab located in the filling spout.

- If the level has dropped, check the entire cooling system for leakage (radiator, hoses, etc.). If there is no leakage, top up the coolant.
- Fill the reserve tank (2, Fig. 5.6a) with coolant up to the **FULL** line mark for coolant top-up.

**Fig. 5.6a callouts:** (1) Radiator cap; (2) Reserve / expansion tank with FULL line mark.

**Sub-zero antifreeze ratio (use Glysantin G40 antifreeze with water):**

| Temperature Range °C [°F] | Antifreeze (%) |
|---|:---:|
| 0 to −3 [32 to 26.6] | 10 |
| −3 to −8 [26.6 to 17.6] | 20 |
| −8 to −16 [17.6 to 3.2] | 30 |
| −16 to −25 [3.2 to −13] | 40 |
| −25 to −37 [−13 to −34.6] | 50 |
| −37 to −55 [−34.6 to −67] | 60 |

*(Table 5.6.1)*

### 5.6.2 Radiator Draining & Flushing (When cold)

1. Remove the radiator cap and drain plug (Fig. 5.6b).
2. Let the coolant drain out. Close the drain cock and plugs. Flush the cooling system with water / cleaning solution for **15 minutes**, then drain the cleaning solution.
3. Refit the drain plug and refill the coolant (mixture of water, anti-scaling agent, antifreeze).
4. Run the engine with the radiator cap open and accelerate 2–3 times; top up coolant if required.
5. Refit the radiator cap and ensure tightness of all connections to check for any leakage.

**Fig. 5.6b:** Shows the **Drain Plug** location at the bottom of the radiator.

### 5.6.3 Radiator Fins Cleaning (Fig. 5.6c)

1. Check radiator fins for holes or cracks / for choking.
2. To clean the radiator, blow compressed air from the engine side to the outside.

### 5.6.4 Radiator Mesh Cleaning (Fig. 5.6d)

1. Raise the bonnet.
2. Pull out the radiator mesh.
3. Clean straw and dust attached to the radiator mesh.

**Fig. 5.6d:** Shows the **Radiator Mesh** being removed for cleaning.

### 5.6.5 Radiator Cap (Fig. 5.6e)

> **CAUTION:** The cooling system is a closed pressurized system, so do not operate the tractor without the radiator cap, or with a cap with damaged rubber seals **(A)** / a defective release valve, to avoid water loss and engine overheating. **Use genuine radiator cap only.**

**Fig. 5.6e:** Radiator cap showing rubber seal **(A)**.

## 5.8 Air Cleaner Maintenance

**Air Cleaner Components (Fig. 5.8a, b, c):**

| No. | Component |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | Air Filter Housing |
| 2 | Clamp |
| 3 | Cover |
| 4 | Rubber Valve |
| 5 | Air Cleaner Element |
| 6 | Clogging Sensor |

**Important Instructions:**

- Clean the air cleaner element at first 50 hours & then after every 250 hrs of operation, or whenever the choke (clogging) indicator glows on the dashboard.
- Clean the filter element by blowing air from the inside. **Maximum pressure should not exceed 1.6 bar (23 P.S.I).**
- Use a clean cloth to wipe sealing areas of the element.
- After replacing the new filter element, ensure matching of the (◄) mark on the cover with the (►) mark on the air filter housing.
- Ensure proper seating of the filter into the housing before latching the cover. Do not use latches on the cover to force the filter into the air cleaner — that could damage the housing and will void the warranty.
- Ensure proper seating of all rubber rings. Replace damaged ones.
- **Replace the air cleaner element after three cleaning operations or at every 750 hours, whichever is earlier.**

> **IMPORTANT:** NEVER attempt to clean the filter element with exhaust gas from the engine. NEVER ever use oil on a dry filter. NEVER ever use oil, diesel fuel, paraffin or solvents to clean the filter element.

**Figures:** Fig. 5.8a shows the assembled air cleaner with numbered callouts (1 housing, 2 clamp, 3 cover, 4 rubber valve, 6 clogging sensor); Fig. 5.8b shows the air cleaner element (5) being removed/installed; Fig. 5.8c shows the element being cleaned by blowing air from inside.

## 5.9 Clutch Pedal

By pressing the clutch pedal, the motion and power of the engine is disengaged from the gearbox. Release the clutch pedal slowly to transfer engine power to the gearbox.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do not keep your foot on the clutch pedal while the tractor is running. It may cause excessive wear of the clutch and the clutch fails before its life time.

## 5.15 Oil Changes for Transmission, Rear Final Drives and Power Lift Hydraulic Circuits

> **WARNING:** Beware of powerful oil jets. Follow all safety rules.

> **NOTE:** When draining out and filling oil and checking oil level, take care that the transmission is in the horizontal position.

**Oil draining:**

1. Lower the lift arms to the ground.
2. Unscrew all the plugs.
3. Place a vessel under all drain plugs of the transmission housing to collect the oil as it drains out.
4. Remove the plugs and drain out the oil.
5. Clean the plugs and fit them back on.

**Filling up the transmission:**

1. Fill up the transmission oil from filler plug **(A)** opening to the maximum level mark on the dipstick.
2. Let it run on idle until the oil reaches a temperature over **25 °C [77 °F]**.
3. Check that the transmission oil reaches the required level mark on the dipstick.
4. If required, fill up to the correct level.

> **NOTE:** Let the oil stabilize before checking its level.

> **NOTE:** If the implement used requires more quantity of oil, make sure the transmission contains enough oil for every work condition. Top up as required.

**Fig. 5.15a:** Shows the transmission filler plug **(A)** and the dipstick **Max mark**.

### 5.17 Cleaning of Suction Strainer (Fig. 5.17a)

At each service, thoroughly clean the suction strainer by washing with light oil or kerosene.

> **WARNING / consequence:** Failure to observe this will result in extensive shortening of the life of the hydraulic system.

**Cleaning Procedure of Suction Strainer (Fig. 5.17b, c, d):**

1. Remove all hose worm clips (1).
2. Separate the hose pipes (2) from the strainer assembly.
3. Hold the strainer assembly in the left hand and remove the wire clip (5) and magnetic strainer (4) from the housing (3) with the help of the right-hand fingers.
4. Unscrew the nut (6) and remove the supporting cup (7). After disassembling the supporting cup, remove the sheath (8) having ferrous dust by sliding it with the help of the plastic support (10).
5. Clean the sheath of ferrous dust with the help of a soft cloth and refit the same.
6. Assemble the supporting cup and tighten the nut.
7. Assemble the magnetic strainer in the strainer housing and lock it with the wire clip.
8. Fix the hose pipes and tighten the hose worm clips.

**Suction strainer component callouts (Fig. 5.17b/c/d):**

| No. | Component |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | Hose worm clips |
| 2 | Hose pipes |
| 3 | Housing |
| 4 | Magnetic strainer |
| 5 | Wire clip |
| 6 | Nut |
| 7 | Supporting cup |
| 8 | Sheath (collects ferrous dust) |
| 9 | Magnets (assembled in polarity sequence) |
| 10 | Plastic support |

> **Replacement:** Replace the magnetic strainer at every 750 Hours.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do not dismantle the magnets (9) as these are assembled in polarity sequence and it should not be disturbed (Fig. 5.17d).

## 5.18 General Maintenance of Electrical System

- **Never patch up the electrical circuits.**
- **Never replace a blown fuse with a higher capacity fuse. It could cause a fire.**
- **Never work on components such as the alternator or starter motor when the engine is running.**
- Lastly, when cleaning the tractor and using a pressure spray, take care not to damage the connections on the various electrical cables.

## 5.19 Battery and its Maintenance

The battery is located at the front of the tractor.

**Battery Removal Procedure:**

1. Open the bonnet.
2. Remove the fly nut by rotating it anticlockwise.
3. Detach the (−)ve and (+)ve terminals (Fig. 5.19a) respectively.

> **WARNING:** Battery posts, terminals, and related accessories contain lead and lead compounds, chemicals known to the State of California to cause cancer and reproductive harm. Wash hands after handling.

> **WARNING:** Remember to disconnect the cables before you recharge the battery. It is advisable to remove the battery from its location and to recharge it well away from the tractor. The place of battery recharge must be well ventilated.

**Check Electrolyte Level:** It must be as per the recommendation of the battery manufacturer. If required, top up with distilled water just touching the 'Max' mark on the battery (Fig. 5.19b). Electrolyte level should never be below the 'Min' mark. **Never add acid.**

**Check Carefully — Battery Charging:** Protect against freezing. Ensure terminals are clean and tight. Check the specific gravity of the battery using a battery hydrometer (Fig. 5.19c). **Specific gravity of a fully charged battery is 1.265 ±0.005 at 27 °C [80.6 °F].**

## 5.21 Alternator

The alternator is fitted on the **left side of the engine** and generates current which charges the battery for healthy electrical back-up.

- Visually check the alternator for damage. If the alternator is dusty, blow off the dust using compressed air.
- Remove the V-belt and turn the pulley by hand to make sure it rotates smoothly.

> **NOTE:** If defects are found in the alternator, contact your dealer.

**Fig. 5.21a:** Photo of the alternator fitted on the left side of the engine.

### 5.21.1 Checking V-belt

1. Ensure the V-belt is free from defects such as wear, cuts or surface separations; otherwise replace with the genuine specified belt.
2. Inspect belt tension by pushing the belt downward with approximately **98 N (10 kgf) (22 lbf)** force midway between the pulleys. If the deflection is **10 to 12 mm [0.39 to 0.47 in]**, the tension is correct. If the tension is out of the specified value, adjust the belt tension.

**Fig. 5.21b:** Shows the **Adjusting bolt** and the **Deflection: 10–12 mm** measurement point.

### 5.21.2 Adjusting V-belt tension

1. Loosen all retaining bolts of the alternator and adjusting plate.
2. Insert a bar between the alternator and cylinder block and use leverage to move the alternator to obtain proper V-belt tension.
3. While the V-belt tension is appropriate, retighten all the retaining bolts of the alternator and adjusting plate.

**Fig. 5.21c callouts:** Adjusting plate, Fixing bolt, Fixing nut.

## 5.23 Long Idle Period

> **CAUTION:** Take the following precautionary measures when your tractor is not going to be used for a long period of time.

- Park the tractor in a dry, sheltered and possibly ventilated place.
- Drain the coolant from the radiator and engine.
- Grease all points provided with grease nipples.
- Remove the injectors and squirt a small quantity of engine oil into the cylinders. Turn the engine over by hand, and then fit the injectors back in place.
- Generally clean the tractor, particularly the bodywork components.
- Protect the painted parts by applying silicon wax and the unpainted metal parts by applying protective lubricant.
- Make sure all the controls are in neutral (including the electric switches and parking brake controls).
- Remove the ignition key from the ignition switch.
- Make sure the cylinder stems (of the power steering, power lift system, etc.) are positioned (retracted).
- Empty the fuel tank and fill it with new diesel fuel until the maximum level is reached.
- Remove the battery, clean the cover and spread Vaseline on the terminals and terminal caps. Connect the battery in a ventilated place where the temperature is not liable to drop below 10 °C and where it is not exposed to direct sunlight.
- Check the battery charge with a voltmeter as described in the battery part of this section. Recharge if necessary.
- Place stands or other supports under the axles in order to take the weight off the wheels. When the tractor is raised in this way, it is advisable to deflate the tyres. If this is not possible, the tyre pressure must be periodically checked.
- Cover the tractor with a tarpaulin (not plastic or waterproof).
- At the end of the idle period, when you start the engine again, pay particular attention to the instructions about starting the engine in the operation chapter.
