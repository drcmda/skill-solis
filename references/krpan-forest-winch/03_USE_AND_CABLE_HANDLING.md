## 5.1 Description (POPIS)

The winch (naviják) is designed for clearing/tidying the forest (i.e. extracting felled timber). It is assembled from a **welded housing/cover (svařovaný kryt)**, the **main shaft (hlavní hřídel)**, a **gear wheel with clutch (ozubené kolo se spojkou)**, a **drum with wire rope (buben s ocelovým lanem)**, a **brake (brzda)**, and the **distributor/divider pulley (řemenice rozdělovače)**.

Using the wire rope (ocelové lano) the timber is dragged into the **collector / butt plate area (sběrník)** and is fastened with **choker/binding ropes (vázací lana)** onto the **grooves/slots on the winch housing (drážky na krytu navijáku)**. The timber can then be transported to a place accessible by other means of transport.

**Main labelled parts / features:**

- **Welded housing (svařovaný kryt)** — the structural body of the winch.
- **Main shaft (hlavní hřídel)** — carries the drive.
- **Gear wheel with clutch (ozubené kolo se spojkou)** — engages the drum drive.
- **Drum with wire rope (buben s ocelovým lanem)** — stores and pulls the rope.
- **Brake (brzda)** — proportional brake (see below).
- **Distributor pulley (řemenice rozdělovače)**.
- **Butt/skid plate (deska)** — formed inward with two bevelled sides and a bevelled bottom face, so the front ends of the logs do not dig in during pulling and slide toward the centre. The plate edges are now rounded so the timber is not damaged during ramping/loading or pushing-off.
- **Top triangular (pyramidal) support structure with guide pulleys (kladky)** — see below.

**Proportional brake.** The serially-fitted proportional brake (proporcionální brzda) on all mechanical winches represents the highest possible safety standard in the forest-winch segment. It gives full control over the speed at which the wire rope is released, preventing jolting of the winch and tractor and unwanted slackening of the pulling rope on the drum. **If the tractor is lifted into the air by the winch during skidding, it can be lowered to the ground at a controlled speed.** The clutch-engagement mechanism is mounted inside the winch and is protected from damage; the brake mechanism is also safely housed inside the casing. By increasing the number of clutch plates (lamely spojky) and optimising the mechanism, the stroke was shortened and the force needed to engage the clutch and release the brake was reduced.

**Pyramidal support structure.** On the mechanically-operated winches all control elements (pull engagement, brake engagement) are protected from external influences and damage inside the pyramidal support structure. The upper, triangular part of the support structure is equipped with guide pulleys (kladky).

**Model range.** There are 4 models in the mechanical-winch line: **4 EP, 5 ERP, 6 ERP, and 8 ERP.**

> **NOTE:** The maximum pulling forces / maximum pulling masses of the individual models are **5 kN (0.5 t) lower** than the tonnage designation in the model name.

> **NOTE:** The letter **R** denotes that the drive runs via an intermediate drive (reductor / reduktor); the letter **P** denotes proportional brake operation.

## 5.2 Required Tractor Equipment (POTŘEBNÉ VYBAVENÍ TRAKTORU)

- The winch may only be driven by a tractor **PTO / cardan shaft (kardanová hřídel)** at **max. 540 rpm (540 min⁻¹)**.
- **Three-point linkage hitch bars (tříbodové připínací tyče)** of:
  - **Category II or III** → 6–8 ERP
  - **Category I or II** → 5 ERP
  - **Category I** → 4 EP

## 5.3 Attaching to the Tractor (PŘIPOJENÍ NA TRAKTOR)

> 🛑 **DANGER:** **When attaching the winch, no one may be in the dangerous vicinity!** (Při připojování navijáku se nikdo nesmí nacházet v nebezpečné blízkosti!)

The forest winch can be attached to any tractor with a three-point hitch, whether with a frame of:

| Model | Three-point linkage category |
| --- | --- |
| 4 EP | Category I |
| 5 ERP | Category I or II |
| 6–8 ERP | Category II or III |

Owing to its corresponding construction, attachment to a tractor with an **automatic coupling frame (automatický přípojný rám)** is also possible.

**Procedure / requirements:**

- Use the **prescribed PTO / cardan shaft (kardanová hřídel)**, with the **shaft guard secured by a retaining/anchor chain (závěsné lano)**. Be attentive that the cardan shaft **clicks/locks into place at both coupling points**.
- To transmit torque from the tractor to the winch, the cardan shaft must be used, and it must correspond to the **recommended tractor power**:

| Model | Recommended tractor power |
| --- | --- |
| 4 EP | 18–35 kW / 25–48 HP |
| 5 ERP | 24–50 kW / 33–68 HP |
| 6 ERP | 33–55 kW / 45–75 HP |
| 8 ERP | 52–99 kW / 70–135 HP |

- After attaching the winch to the tractor, fit the **stabilisers (stabilizátory)** on the lower hitch bars.
- Using the **upper hitch bar (horní přípojná tyč / top link)**, tilt the winch **approximately 20° backward**.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** It is then necessary to **limit the lift travel of the tractor's arms**, to prevent damage to the winch itself and to the PTO/output shaft if the winch is raised too high!

**Definitions:**

- **Three-point linkage (tříbodový závěs)** — the connection to the tractor that, in the case of a mounted machine (the winch), attaches at three points.
- **Category number (I., II., III.)** — denotes the thickness of the pin/bolt (svorník) used to mount the machine (winch).

**Coupling-pin diameters by category** (from the table on p.16):

| Category | Upper pin diameter (horní svorník) | Lower pin diameter (spodní svorník) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| I | Ø 19 mm | Ø 22 mm |
| II | Ø 25 mm | Ø 28 mm |
| III | Ø 32 mm | Ø 36 mm |

## 5.4 Unwinding the Wire Rope (ODVÍJENÍ OCELOVÉHO LANA)

After successfully attaching the winch to the towing vehicle (tractor), the rope can be unwound. The principle is simple: **pulling the red cord (červená šňůra) releases the brake** and the rope can be unwound out to the log/trunk.

> **IMPORTANT:** The pulling rope must always remain firmly wound on the drum — **before starting work with the winch the rope must be fully unwound and then more firmly wound back onto the drum!!!** (When unwinding the rope, **3 wraps/coils of rope (3 cívky lana) must remain on the drum.**) **At the same time, check the integrity of the rope!**

This (firm re-winding) can be done in two ways:

- by **pulling a load**, or
- by **fastening the pulling rope to a stable object and towing the tractor toward that object**.

## 5.5 Skidding / Towing the Load (VLEK)

- Set the **tractor's hydraulic lift to the lower position**. This stabilises the winch via the (butt/skid) plate (deska), and additionally stabilise the tractor with the **parking/hand brake (ruční brzda)**.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **Under no circumstances may you begin skidding before stabilisation!** (V žádném případě nesmíme začít s vlekem před stabilizací!)

- Pull the **grey cord (šedá šňůra)** and move the **winch lever (páka navijáku)** to its **extreme left position**. The rope begins to wind onto the drum. If you release this cord, the rope stops and the **brake immediately stops the drum and holds the load**.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **Any interference with the triggering/actuating mechanism that would prevent operation of the winch clutch and brake is forbidden.** (Je zakázán jakýkoli zásah do spouštěcího mechanizmu, který by zabránil chodu navijákové spojky a brzdy.)

**Operating the pull (Figure 9):** The winch begins to pull when the **grey cord of the clutch lever (šedé lanko spojkové páky)** is pulled. The **clutch lever (páka spojky)** must be pulled **aggressively into its extreme position — until the lever tilts against the stop (mezník) on the winch frame.** In this position the winch has the **highest pulling force**. After releasing the clutch lever (grey cord), the brake automatically stops the winch drum.

**Figure 9 labelled controls and callouts:**

- **Brake lever (brzdová páka)** — actuated by the **red cord (červená čára/šňůra)**. Callout: *"The load is released when the brake lever is moved to its extreme RIGHT position, which is done by pulling the red cord."* (Břemeno se uvolní, když se brzdová páka přesune do krajní pravé polohy, což se provede zatažením za červenou šňůru.)
- **Clutch lever (páka spojky)** — actuated by the **grey cord (šedá čára/šňůra)**. Callout: *"The winch begins to pull when the clutch lever is in its extreme LEFT position. This is achieved by pulling the grey cord; when we stop pulling, the **automatic differential brake (automatická diferenciální brzda)** holds the load."* (Naviják začne táhnout, když je páka spojky v krajní levé poloze. Toho se dosáhne zatažením za šedou šňůru, když přestaneme tahat, automatická diferenciální brzda náklad udrží.)
- Summary: pull the **grey** line (clutch lever to extreme left) to engage the clutch / pull; pull the **red** line (brake lever to extreme right) to release the brake for unwinding; releasing the clutch (grey) lets the brake automatically stop the drum.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **When pulling the grey line, it is forbidden to simultaneously pull the red line!** (Při tažení šedé čáry je zakázáno současně táhnout červenou čáru!)

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **During skidding it is forbidden to raise the tractor's hydraulic linkage!** (Během vlečení je zakázáno zvedat hydraulický závěs traktoru!)

## 5.6 Mounting the Rope (MONTÁŽ LANA)

1. First remove the **front protective cover (přední ochranný kryt)**.
2. Rotate the drum (buben) into a position that allows the **screw on the drum (šroub na bubnu)** to be unscrewed/accessed.
3. Insert the rope into the **guide of the upper pulley (vedení horní kladky)** and, over the upper pulley, lead it into the rope drum.
4. Place the rope into the **groove (drážka)** and tighten the screw.
5. Then begin winding according to the procedure for skidding (see §5.5).
6. When the entire length of rope has been wound on, unwind it once more and re-wind it according to the correct procedure for **firm winding (§5.7)** — because incorrect handling of the pulling rope can shorten its service life.
7. Take particular care that **no loops (smyčky) form** during winding.

> **IMPORTANT:** Incorrect handling of pulling ropes shortens their service life. Take particular care that **no loops form during winding!**

**Figure 10 — mounting sequence (labelled callouts):**

1. Remove the front protective cover.
2. Rotate the drum into the position that allows unscrewing the drum screw.
3. Insert the rope into the guide of the upper pulley and lead it over the upper pulley into the rope drum.
4. Insert the rope into the groove and tighten the screw.

---

## 5.7 Firmly Winding the Rope on the Drum (PEVNÉ NAVÍJENÍ LANA NA BUBEN)

1. First, **fully unwind the rope.**
2. Then wind it onto the drum by **pulling the grey cord (šedá šňůra)**, taking care that the rope is wound **firmly** onto the drum.

This can be done in two ways:

- by **pulling a load**, or
- by **fastening the pulling rope to a stable object and towing the tractor toward that object**.

> **IMPORTANT:** The pulling rope must always remain firmly wound on the drum — before starting work with the winch the rope must be fully unwound and then more firmly wound back onto the drum! (When unwinding the rope, **3 coils of rope must remain on the drum.**) At the same time, check the integrity of the rope!

## 5.8 Rope Integrity (NEPORUŠENOST LANA)

- **A rope can only be claimed/returned under warranty if it has not yet been used!** (Reklamovat je možné pouze lano, které ještě nebylo použito!)
- The pulling rope **must not be longer than the maximum length stated in the technical data.**
- When handling the rope, take care that **no loops form during winding or unwinding.**

**Figure (CORRECT vs INCORRECT winding):**

- **SPRÁVNĚ (CORRECT):** the rope is wound in neat, tight, parallel turns with no slack or crossing.
- **NESPRÁVNĚ (INCORRECT):** the rope forms loose loops / a loop forms that, under load, will tighten and deform the rope.

**Pull-force setting:** The force for unwinding/pulling out the rope must be set correctly so that the drum **stops immediately** after the rope is finally pulled out. This prevents the rope from slackening on the drum on its own.

> **IMPORTANT:** Every new winch is already **factory-set to the maximum pulling force**, which is also recorded in the winch specification table (tabulka tipů/typů k navijáku — [lit.: "table of winch tips/types"]; i.e. the per-model technical-data table). **Increasing the pulling force above these values is not permitted.**

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **Adjustment of the clutch may only be performed by an authorised service, according to the manufacturer's instructions, and recorded in the service book!** (Seřízení spojky může provést pouze autorizovaný servis podle pokynů výrobce a zapsat do servisní knížky!)

> **NOTE:** The wire-rope manufacturer and Vitli Krpan carry out quality control of the wire ropes. **Replacement of wire ropes is not covered by the warranty!** (Na výměnu ocelových lan se záruka nevztahuje!)

## 5.9 Expert Guide to Wire Ropes (ODBORNÝ NÁVOD K OCELOVÝM LANŮM)

### 5.9.1 Wire-rope construction (Složení ocelového lana)

A classic wire rope (ocelové lano) is an indispensable element of a machine or device.

**Construction (from the labelled cutaway diagram):**

- **Steel (ocel)** — the base material; individual **wires (drát)** are drawn from it.
- **Wires (drát)** are wound, in one or more layers, around a **central wire (prostřední drát)**, forming a spiral cord or **strand (pramen)**.
- **Strands (pramen)**, wound in long helices/arcs around the **core (jádro / lano)**, form the finished **wire rope (lano)**.

### 5.9.2 Unwinding, storage and transport of wire ropes (Odvíjení, skladování a přeprava ocelových lan)

This section is shown as a set of "Richtig / Falsch" (correct / incorrect) pictograms. (German labels appear on the source images: **Richtig = správně = correct; Falsch = špatně = incorrect**.)

- **CORRECT (Richtig):** Unwind the rope from a reel by **rolling the reel** (or rotating it on a spindle/turntable) so the rope comes off straight, without twisting. Store reels properly supported.
- **INCORRECT (Falsch):** Pulling the rope off the **side of a stationary coil/reel** (letting it spiral off the end) — this **induces a twist** into the rope per turn and creates loops/kinks. Do not drag the rope off a coil lying flat.

### 5.9.3 Measuring the wire-rope diameter (Měření průměru ocelového lana)

Shown as "Falsch / Richtig" caliper pictograms:

- **INCORRECT (Falsch):** Measuring **across two valleys (between strands)** — i.e. caliper jaws seated in the grooves between strands — gives a false (too-small) reading.
- **CORRECT (Richtig):** Measure the **true outside diameter across the crowns of opposite strands** — i.e. over the widest cross-section so the caliper spans the tops of two strands (effectively the circumscribed circle of the rope).

### 5.9.4 Before first use (Před prvním použitím)

The wire rope of a new winch must first be **"broken in"/stretched (natáhnout)** — that is, **unwound and then wound back under load** with the help of the winch. The winch develops its greatest power at the **first turns (innermost wraps)**, which means you must always **unwind the wire rope fully to the end** and only then begin using the winch. Otherwise the wire rope is **crushed and destroyed on the lower / inner wraps (spodní resp. vnitřní závity).**

> **NOTE:** For longer rope service life, this procedure is recommended to be **repeated daily.**

> **IMPORTANT:** Before using the winch, unwind the rope to the length of the **last 3 turns** and wind it back **under load** (see §5.7 Firmly Winding the Rope on the Drum). **The warranty does not cover damage to wire ropes caused by improper use!**

## 5.10 Damage Caused by Improper Use (POŠKOZENÍ VZNIKLÁ NESPRÁVNÝM POUŽÍVÁNÍM)

> 🛑 **DANGER:** **Correct use of the wire rope can save your life!** (Správné používání ocelového lana vám může ochránit život!)

Each subsection below is a two-column figure table from the manual: **DAMAGE (POŠKOZENÍ)** = a close-up photo of the rope defect; **CAUSE OF DAMAGE (PŘÍČINA POŠKOZENÍ)** = the explanation. The photos are converted to text descriptions below.

### 5.10.1 Damage due to rotation (twisting) — Poškození z důvodu rotace (zkroucení)

| # | Damage (photo description) | Cause of damage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | A wire rope after the **outer strands have "peeled" / unlaid (oloupání vnějších pramenů)**; clearly visible **bunching/build-up of excess length** of the strands. | Due to rotation (twisting) **in the lay direction** of the rope, the "non-rotating" (rotation-resistant) rope **shortened and the core lengthened**. |
| 2 | Inner strands **forced/pushed outward**, protruding through the outer layer. | Wire ropes with **double parallel-laid strands** are sensitive to twisting; here the **inner strands lengthened**, which pushed them outward. |
| 3 | A single inner strand lying **stretched taut along the surface** of the rope. | **Frequent twisting affects only the innermost (shortest) strand** — that strand alone lies stretched along the rope. |
| 4 | On a **six-strand rope**, the **outer wire layers of the outer strands have loosened/slackened**. | Caused by **twisting** of the rope. |
| 5 | The rope **deformed into a "drill-bit"/auger shape** (corkscrew form). | Because the rope **rubbed against parts of the structure**, or was **dragged through grooves that were too narrow**. |
| 7 *(p.22)* | Core strands **excessively lengthened**; damage typically found at the **ends of the rope's range of travel** where the rope runs over pulley blocks or drums. | **Twisting of the rope around the pulley block (kladkostroj)** over-lengthened the strands in the rope core. |
| 8 *(p.22)* | An **open loop** that, **unloaded, forms a loop in the lay (closing) direction**; under load the loop tightens and can **permanently change the rope's shape**. | The rope was **twisted in the opening (unwinding) direction.** |
| 9 *(p.22)* | An **open loop** that, **unloaded, forms a loop in the unwinding direction**. | The rope was **twisted in the lay (winding/rotation) direction.** |

### 5.10.2 Damage caused by pulley blocks / sheaves (Poškození způsobené kladkostroji)

| # | Damage (photo description) | Cause of damage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 10 | Surface wear/flattening along one side of the rope. | This wire rope worked in a **pulley/sheave that was too narrow.** |
| 11 | Similar one-sided crushing/wear of the rope. | Another example of a rope **led through a pulley block that was too narrow.** |
| 12 | **Rapid breakage of wires on the contact surface** of the rope. | A **pulley/sheave that is too wide poorly supports the wire rope**, causing fast wire breakage at the contact face. |

> **NOTE:** Sheave groove sizing matters in both directions — **too narrow** pinches and deforms the rope; **too wide** under-supports it and breaks wires. The groove must match the rope diameter.
