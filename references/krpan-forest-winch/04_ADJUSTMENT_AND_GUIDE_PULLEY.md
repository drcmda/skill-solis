# 04 — Adjustment & Lower Guide Pulley

> **Source:** KRPAN® 4 EP / 5, 6, 8 ERP Single-Drum Forest Winch — Operating Manual (Návod k obsluze / "Návod na použití a bezpečnou práci"), KRPAN d.o.o. (Slovenia), revision 03.02.2025 — translated from the Czech edition — pp.25–28.

This section covers the three field-adjustable transmission/braking elements of the single-drum mechanical forest winch — the clutch (spojka), the pre-brake (předbrzda), and the self-acting differential brake (brzda) — and the correct use of the lower guide pulley (spodní vodicí kladka) for redirecting the steel cable (ocelové lano). The winch is mounted on the tractor's three-point linkage and driven by the PTO / cardan shaft (kardanová hřídel) at 540 rpm.

## 6. Adjustment (Nastavení)

### 6.1 Clutch (Spojka)

Correct adjustment of the clutch (spojka) ensures the optimal pulling/towing force (vlečná síla). The clutch is set at the factory, so no further adjustment is required.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** If the clutch starts to slip (proklouzávání spojky), it is necessary to **interrupt pulling immediately and check whether the load is too heavy!**

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Adjustment of the clutch may be performed **only by an authorized service centre, according to the manufacturer's instructions, and must be recorded in the service booklet (servisní knížka)!**

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** The warranty does **not** cover clutch friction plates/discs (lamely spojky) that are smeared with oil or burnt!

---

### 6.2 Pre-brake (Předbrzda)

Use the screw with wing nut (šroub s křídlovou maticí — see Figure 11) to set the force that prevents the take-up drum (navíjecí buben) from unwinding excessively. Tightening the nut prevents the adjusting screw (stavěcí šroub) from coming loose. Correct adjustment ensures that the cable (lano) unwinds from the drum automatically — that is, not too easily. (Too-free unwinding, combined with a sudden release of the brake while paying out, would damage the cable.)

The force is set correctly when it is **still possible to unwind the cable without great effort.** When pulling the cable uphill (do svahu), this brake must be loosened further to make paying out the cable easier.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** You must pull the steel cable (ocelové lano) **evenly, without jerky movements**, which could cause the cable to swing/oscillate on the drum and form loops (smyčky).

**Figure 11 — Pre-brake adjustment.** The illustration shows the assembled winch (with the protective mesh guard) and a detail of the pre-brake adjuster on the drum side. Its callout reads:

> **Turning the screw to the right increases the force, while turning the screw to the left decreases the pre-tension/pre-load force.** *(Czech: "Otáčením šroubu doprava se síla zvyšuje, zatímco otáčením šroubu doleva se síla předpětí snižuje.")*

| Adjuster action | Effect |
|---|---|
| Turn the screw to the right (clockwise) | Increases the force / pre-load — cable harder to pay out |
| Turn the screw to the left (counter-clockwise) | Decreases the pre-tension (předpětí) force — cable easier to pay out |
| Tighten the wing/lock nut against the adjusting screw | Locks the setting — prevents the adjusting screw (stavěcí šroub) from working loose |

> **NOTE:** The pre-brake (předbrzda) is set at the factory, but each operator may set their own braking force to suit their work.

### 6.3 Brake (Brzda)

When you interrupt pulling, the self-acting differential brake (samovolná diferenciální brzda) holds the load — the drum (buben) does not rotate back, so the pulling cable stays taut. The brake is factory-set. Over time, however, as the material beds in (settles) or the friction lining (třecí obložení) wears, the brake may no longer hold or may slip. If you find that the brake will not hold the pulled load, the differential brake must be readjusted.

To readjust the brake, follow the steps below:

1. Loosen the screws on the front protective cover (přední ochranný kryt) and then remove it. Inside the forest winch is the wrench/key for adjusting the brake nut (klíč na seřízení brzdové matice); it is fastened to the winch housing with two screws — to use it, loosen these two screws.
2. Using the wrench, loosen the lower nut (spodní matice).
3. Tighten or loosen the upper nut (horní matice) until the gap (mezera) is **8 mm**.
4. Tighten or loosen the lower nut until it is **15 mm** from the upper nut. If necessary, loosen or tighten the nut as shown in Figure 12.1.
5. After adjusting all the brake nuts as required, screw the brake-nut adjustment wrench back into its place, then refit and screw down the front protective cover.

**Figure 12 — Differential brake adjustment.** The illustration shows the brake spring/nut stack with three magnified detail views. The figure's own text callouts are:

- **Step 1 (Krok 1):** "Unscrew the screws on the front protective cover and remove it." *(Czech: "Odšroubujte šrouby na předním ochranném krytu a sejměte jej.")*
- **The brake-adjustment wrench is supplied with the unit (součástí dodávky)** — shown stowed against the winch housing.
- **Figure 12.1 (Obrázek 12.1):** "Using the supplied wrench (dodaný klíč), loosen or tighten the nut as needed." *(Czech: "Pomocí dodaného klíče matici podle potřeby povolte nebo utáhněte.")*

The remaining steps and their measurements come from the numbered procedure above:

| Step | Action | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Loosen the screws on the front protective cover and remove it | — |
| 2 | Loosen the lower nut (spodní matice) with the wrench | — |
| 3 | Tighten/loosen the upper nut (horní matice) until the gap is **8 mm** | gap = **8 mm** |
| 4 | Adjust the lower nut until it is **15 mm** from the upper nut | **15 mm** from upper nut |

> **NOTE:** The brake-nut adjustment wrench/key (klíč na seřízení brzdových matic) is supplied with the winch and is stored fastened to the winch housing (two screws) behind the front protective cover.

## 7. Using the Lower Guide Pulley (Použití spodní vodicí kladky)

The lower guide pulley (spodní vodicí kladka / řemenice) redirects the steel cable when the load cannot be pulled in a straight line to the winch. Figure (p.28) shows the tractor-mounted winch with the cable routed out through the lower guide pulley, around an anchor point, to logs lying on the ground, illustrating both correct routing and the hazards below.

When using the guide pulley, observe the following:

- **Match the pulley to the cable.** When using the guide pulley, pay attention to the correct/corresponding diameter of the sheave (průměr kotouče) for the cable being used.
- **Keep end-stops/sliders clear of the pulley.** During pulling, the cable-stop sleeves / chokers (omezovací obklady — cable end fittings/stops) or the sliders/runners (posuvníky) **must not** be drawn into the pulley.

> 🛑 **DANGER:** **STAY AT A SAFE DISTANCE!** (POHYBUJTE SE V BEZPEČNÉ VZDÁLENOSTI!) Keep clear of the danger zone of the cable under tension and of the guide pulley while pulling.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** When pulling over the lower guide pulley (spodní řemenice), the slider/runner (jezdec) must **never** be drawn into the lower pulley. If this happens, the cable (lano) or the lower pulley (spodní kladka) may be damaged.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** When using sliders/runners (posuvníky), be attentive **not to pull the cable through the slider at a sharp angle** (v ostrém úhlu).
