# 07 — Cleaning, Transport, Disconnection & Storage

> **Source:** KRPAN® 4 EP / 5, 6, 8 ERP Single-Drum Forest Winch — Operating Manual (Návod k obsluze / "Návod na použití a bezpečnou práci"), KRPAN d.o.o. (Slovenia), revision 03.02.2025 — translated from the Czech edition — pp.37–41.

This reference covers the end-of-work and ownership tasks for the single-drum forest winch (jednobubnový lesní mechanický naviják): cleaning the machine and its interior, transporting it (by crane/forklift and on the road), disconnecting it from the tractor's three-point linkage and PTO, and long-term storage. The winch is mounted on the tractor's three-point hitch and driven by the PTO / cardan shaft (kardanová hřídel) at 540 rpm; all of these procedures assume the machine is stopped and de-powered first.

## 12. Cleaning (Čištění)

> 🛑 **DANGER — SWITCH THE MACHINE OFF FIRST!!!** (STROJ NEJPRVE VYPNĚTE!!!)
> Before cleaning, the machine MUST be disconnected from its power source (the tractor drive / PTO).

Cleaning notes:

- Do **not** use aggressive cleaning agents that could damage the machine's seals (těsnění) or paint (barva).
- If you use a high-pressure (vysokotlaký) or steam (parní) jet, watch the pressure setting and the distance from which you clean — too high a pressure or too close a nozzle first damages the paint and then leads to corrosion at that spot.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Never clean the bearings (ložiska) or hydraulic components (hydraulické součásti) with a high-pressure or steam jet under any circumstances!

**Figure 19 — Incorrect cleaning (Nesprávné čištění):** A high-pressure / steam jet aimed directly at the winch, marked with a large red **X** — i.e. directing a pressure or steam jet straight at the machine (and especially at bearings and hydraulic parts) is prohibited.

### Inspect the machine after every cleaning!

- After each cleaning you may spot possible damage to individual parts of the machine. If you notice any, remove/repair it **immediately** to prevent damage to the whole machine or injury.
- After each cleaning, lubricate the machine wherever needed to drive out any water that may have penetrated into the machine.
- Work and cleaning can damage the paint; this damage must be repaired to restore corrosion protection.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Cleaning the machine is **not permitted while the machine is running!** Before any intervention near the machine, the machine **must be switched off!**

> **IMPORTANT:** Regular maintenance and cleaning are a precondition for any warranty claim. [lit.: "The condition for claiming the warranty is solely regular maintenance or cleaning."]

### 12.1 Cleaning the winch interior (Čištění vnitřku navijáku)

To clean the inside of the winch (wood residue / zbytky dřeva, mud / bláto):

1. First **stop and let the machine cool down**, so you do not get burned or cause other damage.
2. Remove the **front protective cover/guard (přední ochranný kryt)**.
3. Then remove the **box/housing cover (kryt skříňky)**.
4. Use the **comb/rake (hřeben)** to clear foreign particles out of the inside of the winch through the cleaning opening.
5. After cleaning, **refit the box lid (víko bedny) and the front protective cover** so the winch is not damaged.

**Figure 20 — Winch interior cleaning:** The machine is shown with the front protective guard removed. A red callout box on the right highlights an opening labelled **"Opening for clearing debris" (Otvor pro úklid nečistot)** — this is the access point through which foreign material is raked out. The lower caption reads: *"To clean foreign objects out of the winch, you must remove the lid on the bottom of the box / housing"* (Chcete-li z navijáku vyčistit cizí předměty, je třeba sejmout víko na dně schránky) — i.e. access for raking debris out is through the lid at the **bottom of the housing (víko na dně schránky)**.

> **CAUTION:** Refit both the box lid and the front protective cover after cleaning; running the winch with these covers off can damage it.

## 13. Transporting the machine (Přeprava stroje)

The winch can be transported / lifted above the pick-up point using a **crane (jeřáb)** or a **forklift (vysokozdvižný vozík)** together with approved lifting equipment (povolená zdvihací zařízení).

**Figure 21 — Lifting point:** A leader-line on the left of the frame is labelled **"Lifting point above the pin; the pin must be secured!"** (Zvedací bod nad čepem, čep musí být zajištěn čepem!) — i.e. the designated **lifting point is above the pin/pivot (čep)** at the top of the frame, and that pin must be secured (per the body text, with the cotter pin / split pin (závlačka)) before lifting.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** The machine may only be transported when the winch is in the **transport position (přepravní poloha)** and the pin is **secured with the cotter pin (závlačka)!**

## 14. Disconnecting from the tractor (Odpojení od traktoru)

After finishing work, the winch must be properly disconnected and stored. Before disconnecting, clean the winch and prepare it for the next use.

The order is the **reverse of the connection sequence (see Section 5.3)**:

1. First **unscrew/release the lower links (spodní ojnice)** of the three-point linkage.
2. **Tilt the winch forward again by 20 degrees.**
3. Rest the winch on its **supports (podpěry)**.
4. Disconnect the winch from the **three-point hitch (tříbodová spojka)**.
5. Disconnect the **articulated / cardan (PTO) shaft (kloubový / kardanový hřídel)** as well, and secure it with the bracket/yoke (třmen) or store it separately in a place provided for storing cardan shafts.

**Figure 22 — Disconnection:** The **PTO / cardan shaft guard (kryt kardanového hřídele)** is labelled — disconnect the shaft and secure or store it (its guard protects it during storage).

> **CAUTION:** Disconnect in the reverse order of connection: release the lower links, tilt the winch ~20° forward onto its supports, then free it from the three-point hitch and disconnect/store the PTO shaft.

## 15. Storing the forest winch (Uložení lesnického navijáku)

The forest winch is a key forestry tool, so it must be stored appropriately to ensure long service life and trouble-free operation. Correct storage means protecting it from the weather, preserving the function of its components, and ensuring the winch is stable so it cannot be damaged or become a hazard.

Follow these instructions when storing the forest winch:

- **Dry space (Suchý prostor):** Store the forest winch in a dry place. Avoid storing it in damp locations, where moisture can cause corrosion and component failure.
- **Stable and secure setup (Stabilní a bezpečná instalace):** Make sure the winch is placed on a level, stable surface so it cannot tip over or fall. When setting the winch down, ensure it is well secured and protected against physical damage such as impacts or vibration that could be caused by other equipment or the working environment. Incorrect storage or incorrect placement can lead to damage to the winch or to a hazard during subsequent use.
- **Prevent component damage (Předcházejte poškození součástí):** Regularly check the condition of the components to make sure they are not damaged.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do **not** store the winch in damp/humid spaces, as the sensitive parts could be damaged!

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** The forest winch must be stored in a **stable and protected environment** with enough space for safe placement and no risk of damage. Make sure it is properly protected against the weather to ensure a longer machine service life!

> **NOTE — rope (lano) care:** Storing the winch dry and protecting it from the weather also protects the steel wire rope (ocelové lano) from corrosion; inspect the rope's condition as part of the regular component checks before returning the winch to service.
