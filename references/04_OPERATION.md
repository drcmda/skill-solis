## 4.1 Operator Presence Control (OPC)

The OPC is a safety system that monitors operator presence on the seat and reacts (buzzer alarm and/or automatic engine shut-off) depending on the combination of seat, parking brake, PTO and gear states.

### 4.1.1 Parking Brake OPC

This tractor is equipped with an audible and visible alarm that alerts the operator when he leaves the driving position with the park brake not applied. This audible and visible alarm shall be activated after the operator has been detected out of the driving position and the parking brake is not applied. The time-out of alarm is 5–7 seconds. The alarm shall be deactivated when the operator is detected to be present again in the driving position within this time period, or when the parking brake is applied within this time period.

### 4.1.2 Power Take-off OPC

When the operator leaves the driving position with PTO engaged and the vehicle is not in motion, the engine will shut off automatically, which will shut off the drive of the power take-off shaft within 7 seconds. The automatic PTO shut-off action shall not have negative effects on safety-related functions (e.g. braking). A restart of the power take-off shall only be possible by an intentional actuation of the operator.

> **NOTE:** To start the tractor again, the operator has to put all levers in neutral position and press the brake pedal.

### OPC Feedback Table

| Tractor Condition | PTO Condition | Gears Condition | Seat Condition | Parking Brake Condition | Buzzer | OPC Feedback |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ON | OFF | Neutral | Operator Leaves Seat | OFF | Will Blow | Buzzer will continuously blow until operator sits on the seat |
| ON | ON | Neutral | Operator Leaves Seat | OFF | Will Blow | Buzzer will blow continuously till engine stops within 5–7 seconds |
| ON | ON | Neutral | Operator Leaves Seat | ON | Doesn't Blow | Engine stops within 5–7 seconds |
| ON | ON | Engaged | Operator Leaves Seat | OFF | Will Blow | Buzzer will blow continuously till engine stops within 5–7 seconds |
| ON | ON | Engaged | Operator Leaves Seat | ON | Doesn't Blow | Engine stops within 5–7 seconds |
| ON | OFF | Engaged | Operator Leaves Seat | OFF | Will Blow | Buzzer will blow continuously until operator sits on the seat |
| ON | OFF | Engaged | Operator Leaves Seat | ON | Doesn't Blow | No buzzer, no engine shut-off |

## 4.3 Engine

### 4.3.1 Starting the Engine

The ignition switch is used to start the engine. The switch has the following four positions (see **Fig. 4.3a – Ignition Switch** and **Fig. 4.3b – Ignition Switch Positions**).

**Ignition switch positions:**

| No. | Position | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **OFF** | When the key is turned to this position, power supply to the electric circuits is cut off, and the key can be removed or inserted in this position. |
| 2 | **ON + HEAT** | When the key is turned to this position, power is supplied to the electric circuits. After the engine starts, the key is held in this position. In this position, glow plugs would become hot and allow easy startup of a cold engine. |
| 3 | **START** | When the key is turned to this final position, the starter cranks the engine and the engine starts. When the key is released, it automatically returns to the 'ON' position. |

**For starting (pre-start checks A–E):**

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| A | Check that the gear shifter lever is in neutral. |
| B | Move the range lever to neutral position. |
| C | The tractor is equipped with a clutch safety switch — always press the clutch pedal fully before starting the engine. |
| D | Check that the PTO lever is in neutral. |
| E | Release the hand brake (if engaged). |

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** When the engine is running, keep at a safe distance from the radiator fan.

### 4.3.2 Cold Weather Starting

**(Temperature below 0 °C or 32 °F)**

Proceed as follows:

1. Perform operations **A to C** as instructed above.
2. Turn the ignition key to the 'Heat' position and when the glow plug indicator goes off, then turn the key to the start position.
3. If the engine fails to start, repeat Step 2, wait for a further 5 to 10 seconds, and then turn the key to the start position again.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do not inject starting fluids (ether) to make the engine easier to start in cold weather. The tractor is equipped with a cold start device.

**Additional starting notes:**

- If the engine fails to start after two or three attempts and smoke can be seen coming out of the exhaust, repeat the starting procedure with less time on the glow plug heater.
- Do not keep the key turned to start position for more than **5–8 seconds** at a time.
- Wait at least **one minute** after every two failed attempts of starting the tractor.

If the engine does not start regularly and easily, do not continue, as you may run down the battery. Bleed any air that may have accumulated in the fuel system and, if the problem persists, check that:

1. Fuel filters are not blocked.
2. The battery and heater plugs are working efficiently.

### 4.3.3 Running In

It is essential to take the following precautions during the running-in period:

1. During this period, do not subject the tractor to loads greater than those it will have to deal with during the rest of its working life.
2. Engage low gear when towing heavy loads.
3. When running in, check regularly that all screws, nuts and bolts are tight.

### 4.3.4 Turning Off the Engine

1. Turn the accelerator to idle position.
2. Stop the engine by turning the ignition key to the 'OFF' position.

### 4.3.5 Stopping and Parking

- Reduce the engine speed using the hand throttle lever / accelerator pedal.
- Press the clutch pedal and brake pedal to stop the tractor.
- Move all gear levers to neutral position.
- Turn the ignition key to the 'OFF' position to stop the engine.
- Remove the ignition key.
- Lower any implement to the ground.
- Apply the parking brake.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Always apply the parking brake when parking. Failure to do so can cause accidents and damage. As an extra precaution when parking on a slope, chock the rear wheels.

- **Pedal pressed = Drive disengaged.**

## 4.8 2WD / 4WD Lever

You can drive the tractor in both 2WD or 4WD mode. Select the driving mode by lever as shown in **Fig. 4.8**.

- **4WD mode:** With the lever in the 4WD position (**upward direction**), the power is simultaneously transmitted to all 4 wheels of the tractor.

> **NOTE:** 4WD mode is for field operation and 2WD mode is for road operation.

## 4.12 Power Take Off (PTO)

The power take-off is mounted at the rear left side of the operator. This is used for supplying power directly to an implement from the engine. PTO can be engaged or disengaged by the PTO shifter lever (see **Fig. 4.12a**).

**PTO speed table (Fig. 4.12a — knob shows "540 E" and "540"):**

| Shuttle Lever Position | PTO Speed | Engine RPM |
|---|---|---|
| PTO 1 | 540 | 2565 |
| PTO 2 | 540E | 2083 |

### Rear PTO

In the rear PTO, the PTO speed is **540E RPM**. Engage the rear PTO as per the following steps:

- Make sure that the PTO switch is in OFF position.
- Shift the PTO lever to backward direction.
- Decrease engine speed to idle RPM.
- Turn on the PTO switch.
- Increase engine speed as desired.

> **IMPORTANT:** If the operator turns off the PTO switch, rear PTO and mid PTO are off at once.

> **IMPORTANT:** When PTO is not operational, protect PTO splines with PTO Cap (A). The PTO Cap protects persons from injuries and the shaft splines from damage.

**PTO safety warnings:**

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Before connecting, adjusting or working on implements operated by the PTO, disengage the PTO, stop the engine, remove the key from the dashboard and engage the parking brake. Do not work under raised implements.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** When using the PTO drive with a stationary tractor, ALWAYS make sure that the parking brake is applied.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Before driving an implement through the PTO, ALWAYS make sure that all bystanders are well away from the tractor.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Check to make sure that all implements operated by the PTO are fitted with the correct protections, are in good condition and comply with the provisions established by the law.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Remove PTO cap (A, Fig. 4.12b) only when the PTO is to be used. As soon as a PTO-driven implement is removed, re-install the cap over the PTO stub shaft again afterwards. There are various versions of PTO guard that are not shown here.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Before using the PTO, the maximum permissible angle of articulation on the telescoping driveline must be ascertained. During operation, there must be no contact between the PTO guard and the telescoping driveline. This is particularly important when turning corners.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Never operate the PTO unless the master shield (B) is in the position shown. Switch off the PTO before raising the implement. Always put a guard on the telescoping driveline and take action to prevent it from turning with the shaft. Do not operate the telescoping driveline unless a guard is installed that covers the PTO shaft completely and does not turn with the shaft.

**Figure references (PTO components and pictograms):**

| Fig. | Shows |
|---|---|
| Fig. 4.12b | PTO stub shaft with cap **A** and master shield **B**. |
| Fig. 4.12c | Pictograms — keep clear of rotating PTO shaft; entanglement hazard. |

**Articulation on Telescoping Driveline (Fig. 4.12e):**

As far as possible, angles (1) and (2) at the universal joints should be the same at both ends of the telescoping driveline.

- **1 — Z-shaped layout**
- **2 — W-shaped layout**

In applications where this is not the case (e.g. sharp turns with PTO engaged), it is recommended to use a continuous-velocity drive shaft.

> **NOTE:** The two schematic drawings do not show any guards on the telescoping driveline. A guard is mandatory when using telescoping drivelines.

**Align Forks Correctly:**

> **IMPORTANT:** On multi-component telescoping drivelines, the yokes at each end must be aligned as shown. The yokes at each end must NOT be at 90° to one another.

> **IMPORTANT:** Before using a PTO-driven implement, take action to ensure that the telescoping driveline is lubricated regularly. Comply with the instructions in the Operator's Manual provided by the manufacturer.

**A requirement to use only power take-off drive shafts with adequate guards.**

**Information about using implements with power take-off drive shafts:**

1. Shut off the engine and disengage PTO before attaching PTO-driven equipment. Turn key off to stop engine.
2. Attach the implement to the tractor before connecting the PTO drive line. Lock TPL (three-point linkage) in upward position if it is not to be used.
3. Rotate the PTO shield upward for clearance. With engine off, turn the shaft slightly by hand if necessary to line up splines. Connect the drive line to the PTO shaft. Pull out on the shaft to be sure the drive line is locked to the PTO shaft. Place the PTO shield in the downward position.
4. Be sure all shields are in place and in good condition. Never operate PTO unless the master shield is properly installed. WITH ENGINE STOPPED, check integral shields on the drive line by making sure they rotate freely on the shaft. Lubricate or repair as necessary.
5. Check carefully for any interference; make sure TPL is locked in the upward position if it is not used.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Before attempting to clean, adjust or lubricate a PTO-driven machine or the TPL, always make sure the PTO is switched off and stopped, the tractor engine is shut off and the ignition key is removed.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** High-inertia implements do not come to a standstill the moment the PTO control lever is shifted to the disengaged position. Do NOT approach the implement while it is "coasting down". Do not work on the implement until it has stopped.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Stay clear from the area of the three-point linkage when controlling it.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** The mounted machinery must be lowered on the ground before leaving the tractor.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Stay clear from the area between tractor and trailed vehicle.

### PTO External Control Switch

If you want to operate any PTO-driven implement in static condition of the tractor without sitting on the driver seat, the same can be achieved with the help of the PTO external control switch. This switch is located near the ignition switch (**Fig. 4.12f**). Switch positions: **OFF / ON**.

**Mode of Operation:**

During static condition (operator leaves the seat and PTO is in running condition), engage the parking brake and press the switch in the downward direction to the 'ON' condition. This will prevent the engine from getting shut off.

## 4.13 Hydraulic Coupling Devices

One Double-Acting (1DA) detent-type Direction Control Valve (DCV) is equipped as a standard fitment in your tractor. The DCV is used when attaching the implement operated by hydraulic cylinder. The female Quick Release Couplers (QRC's) are located at the rear side of the tractor (see **Fig. 4.13a**).

**Procedure:**

1. Make sure the hose end and coupler receptacles (male & female) are in perfectly clean condition.
2. Remove the dust plug from the QRC's.
3. To connect the male coupler, push it firmly into the female coupler receptacle. Pull lightly to make sure a positive connection was made.
4. Use the DCV lever (**Fig. 4.13b**) for operating DCV.
5. Move the DCV lever forward or backward to control the implement.

> **CAUTION:** Use cylinder implements only according to the DCV fitted in your tractor.

> **CAUTION:** Hydraulic hoses can fail due to physical damage, kinks, age and exposure. Check hoses regularly. Replace damaged hoses.

> **CAUTION:** Make sure that the DCV lever is in neutral position in case the DCV is not in use. Failure to follow this instruction may result in serious damage of hydraulic components.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Never unhitch the implements before removing the QRC's.

## 4.16 Differential Lock Pedal

The rear axle differential is equipped with a locking device to engage when one of the rear wheels slips owing to lack of grip. To lock the differential, slow the tractor down and fully press the differential lock pedal (**Fig. 4.16**).

The differential must remain locked until the driving wheels regain their grip. To disengage the lock, just remove your foot from the pedal. If the differential fails to release, sharply brake on the wheels. Brake the wheel that is out of the furrow during ploughing work.

> **NOTE:** For the best results, engage the differential lock before the wheels are likely to slip. Do not lock the differential without having first depressed the clutch pedal.

> **IMPORTANT:** Differential lock operation should be in straight position only and should be disengaged at turnings to avoid any damage of differential assembly.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not apply differential lock while tractor speed is more than 6 kmph [3.73 mph] on turning.

## 4.21 Wheels and Tyres

Tyres play a vital role in transportation and agriculture operations. It is the most important factor in the efficient performance of the tractor; it should be used only as per company recommendation. (Here, only pneumatic tyres are discussed.)

**In general, the tractor is considered for two types of work:**

- Work on hard ground and roads, towing, etc. In this case there will be use of **maximum pressure**.
- Work on soft soil where maximum adhesion is needed. In this case there will be use of the **lowest pressure** compatible with the load carried.

**Tyre marking explanation (how to read tyre size):**

On any tyre there is some marking which represents its size & capacity, e.g. tyre marking **8.3 × 20, 4 ply rating**, i.e. **8.3 inch is the section width**, **20 inch is the bead diameter**. Ply rating doesn't show that the same number of plies are inserted in the tyre — it is only a comparative measure of the **Load Carrying Capacity (L.C.C.)** of the tyre. More ply rating shows more L.C.C.; at the same time, as L.C.C. increases the shock absorption capacity decreases.

### In Field Operations

**Recommended Tyre Pressure — Front: 20~22 P.S.I / Rear: 14~16 P.S.I**

| Condition | Effect (Adherence / Wear) |
|---|---|
| **Correct Pressure** | Good adherence by dirt grousers. Good cleaning of the tread. |
| **Insufficient Pressure** | Reduced adherence through lack of tyre grip. Deterioration of tyre casing by traction forces. |
| **Over Pressure** | Reduced grip due to lack of cleaning. Deterioration due to compacted ground. |

### On Haulage Operations

**Recommended Tyre Pressure — Front: 22~24 P.S.I / Rear: 16~18 P.S.I**

| Condition | Effect (Resistance to Wear) |
|---|---|
| **Correct Pressure** | Resistance to wear. |
| **Insufficient Pressure** | Reduced adherence through lack of tyre grip. Deterioration of tyre casing by traction forces. |
| **Over Pressure** | Reduced grip due to lack of cleaning. Deterioration due to compacted ground. |

*(Diagrams show three tyre cross-sections: Correct Pressure — even tread contact; Insufficient Pressure — bulged sidewall / worn shoulders; Over Pressure — domed tread / worn centre.)*

## 4.23 Ballasting the Front Axle

Proper ballasting is an important factor in tractor performance. For better performance, the weight of the tractor can be decreased as per requirement. Maximum productivity can be achieved only if tractor weight is appropriate for the job. Ballast is required for traction and stability. The tractor is equipped with a detachable front toe hook. (**Fig. 4.23** — Front Weights.)

**The following factors determine the amount of ballast (operating conditions):**

| Factor | Variants |
|---|---|
| Soil type / implement | Type of implement |
| Soil surface | Loose or firm |
| Travel speed & power output | Partial or full load |

By default, the tractor is equipped with **2 front weights of 15 kg each, i.e. 30 kg [66.14 pounds]**.

## 4.25 Three Point Linkage

Three-point linkage is used to mount the implement, which is fully mounted or semi-mounted and used for different field operations. The three-point linkage is controlled by the hydraulic lever. In this, two lower links are available, of which one side of the lower link is attached with the differential housing and the other is used to hitch the lower pin of the implement. Lift rods are mounted on the lift arm that is operated through the rockshaft. The loose side of the top link is used for attaching the upper hitch pin of the implement. The top link is adjustable for proper setting of the implement and ease at the time of joining.

**Three Point Linkage components (Fig. 4.25):**

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Top Link | Adjustable upper link; attaches to upper hitch pin of implement. |
| Lift Rod (×4 callouts shown) | Mounted on lift arm; transmits rockshaft motion to lower links. |
| Lower Link (×3 callouts shown) | Attaches to differential housing on one side; hitches implement lower pin on the other. |
| Draw Bar | For drawn/trailed implements. |
| Tow Hook | For towing. |

### Adjustable Lift Rods

The lift rods can be adjusted mechanically to make the lower links level and lined up with each other. This will depend on the type of implement being used and the work to be done. (**Fig. 4.25b** — Lift Rod (LH) and Lift Rod (RH).)

### Top Link

For length adjustment of the top link, fix the top link's other end and turn the lever for increasing or decreasing the length. During field operation, lock the tube to avoid unnecessary turning. (**Fig. 4.25c** — Top Link.)

### Lower Links

Lower links are provided for hitching the implement. (**Fig. 4.25d** — Lower Link (LH) and Lower Link (RH).)

### Attaching Implement to 3 Point Linkage

Position the tractor to align the corresponding linkage with the hitch points of the implements. Keep the implement on a hard & levelled surface and attach as per the instructions below:

- First attach with the **Left lower link** and **Right lower link**.
- Then at last attach with the **Top link**.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Stay clear from the area of the three-point linkages while attachment and detachment of implements.

> **NOTE:** Maximum allowed vertical load on rear hitch is **248 Kg-f [0.55 pound-force]**.

## 4.26 Safety Frame: Roll Over Protection Structure (ROPS)

A safety frame and seat belt is fitted as standard equipment to the platform tractor at the time of factory assembly. If the safety frame was deleted by the original purchaser or has been removed, it is recommended that you equip your tractor with a safety frame and a seat belt. Safety frames are effective in reducing injuries during overturn accidents. (**Fig. 4.26a** — ROPS; **Fig. 4.26b** — Seat Belt.)

The ROPS has been certified to industry and/or government standards. Any damage or alteration to the ROPS, mounting hardware or seat belt voids the certification and will reduce or eliminate the protection for the operator in the event of a roll-over.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** A tractor overturning without a safety frame can result in serious injury or death.

> **IMPORTANT:** Do not weld, drill, bend or straighten the safety frame.

### Operation

- Before using the tractor, ensure that the safety frame is not damaged and that it is securely fastened to the tractor.
- DO NOT ATTACH chains, ropes or cables to the safety frame for pulling purposes; this will cause the tractor to tip backwards. Always pull from the tractor drawbar.
- Always wear your seat belt — adjusted snugly — except when the safety frame has been removed.
- Check the seat belt for damage. A damaged seat belt must be replaced (**Fig. 4.26b**).
- If the safety frame has been removed from the tractor, it must be refitted or erected immediately using the proper hardware and applying the recommended torque value.

### Maintenance and Inspection

The ROPS, mounting hardware and seat belt should be checked at every service for any evidence of damage, wear or cracks. The ROPS has been certified to industry and/or government standards. Any damage or alteration voids the certification and will reduce or eliminate the protection for the operator in the event of a roll-over.

### Normal Operating Position

For normal operation, including transport, always use the ROPS in the upright position with a fastened seat belt for full rollover protection (**Fig. 4.27a**).

### Operating Foldable ROPS

- Unscrew the Hex screw **M10** (1, **Fig. 4.27c**) — 2 Pcs.
- Remove the snap ring (2, **Fig. 4.27d**) — 2 Pcs.
- Remove the Pin (3, **Fig. 4.27d**) — 2 Pcs. from the ROPS bracket.

> **CAUTION:** When raising or folding the ROPS, apply the parking brake, stop the engine and remove the key. To avoid personal injury, hold the ROPS tightly with both hands and fold the ROPS slowly and carefully.

> **CAUTION:** Always perform the function from a stable position at the rear of the tractor. Fold the ROPS down only when absolutely necessary and fold it up and lock it again as soon as possible.

*(Fig. 4.26c shows the folded ROPS with Lift Rod and Lower Link callouts.)*
