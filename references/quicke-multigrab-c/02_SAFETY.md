## 2.1 General Information

Driver safety is one of the most important matters when a new implement is designed. The designer builds in as many safety functions as possible. Several accidents occur every year which could have been avoided by taking a few seconds of consideration and through more careful operation of the implement.

To avoid personal injury, read the following personal safety instructions and insist that everybody who works with you, or for you, also complies with the instructions.

### 2.1.1 Warning and Prohibiting Decals

Warning and prohibiting decals are fitted in various locations on the implement.

- Locate, read and find out what the decals mean **before** using the loader / base machine and implement.
- Do **not** cover or remove any warning or prohibiting decal.
- If a warning or prohibiting decal is missing or illegible, replace it with a new decal. New warning or prohibiting decals can be obtained from your dealer.

The decal labels are clustered on the implement frame; the figure cross-references each pictogram and the machine sign (type plate) to its mounting position so missing items can be identified and reordered.

## 2.2 Explanation of Warning Levels

When you see the safety symbol and the signal words on decals or in the instruction manual, the instructions **MUST** be followed since they are related to your own personal safety.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.6** — Locate, read and find out what the decals mean before using the loader / base machine and implement. (The figure shows the safety triangle symbol used to flag the signal-word notices.)

The signal-word hierarchy used throughout this manual and on the decals is as follows:

| Signal word | Refers to personal safety? | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **DANGER!** | Yes | Indicates an immediately dangerous situation which, if not avoided, **can lead to death or serious personal injury**. |
| **WARNING!** | Yes | Means that an accident could occur if the instruction is not followed. The accident might lead to **serious personal injury or death**. |
| **CAUTION!** | Yes | Means that an accident could occur if the instruction is not followed. An accident could lead to **personal injury**. |
| **IMPORTANT!** | No (operation/service) | Means that an accident could occur if the instruction is not followed. The accident might lead to **damage to the property or to the process, or personal injury**. |
| **NOTE** | No (operation/service) | Refers to extra information which could facilitate the understanding or implementation of a certain task. |

The DANGER / WARNING / CAUTION levels refer to personal safety. The IMPORTANT and NOTE texts do not refer to personal safety but are used consistently in the manual to provide tips about operation or service of the implement.

## 2.3 Supervision and Users

### 2.3.1 Responsibility

> **CAUTION:** Always store this user manual as well as the base machine's own instruction manual in the base machine.

- If there is no user manual included with the base machine, then obtain one from the dealer before fitting and using the implement.
- Read through all material carefully and learn how to use the equipment in a safe and correct manner.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Only use the implement for the intended application area. Do not handle and/or transport items that significantly displace the centre of gravity. **IMPLEMENT WEIGHT plus LOAD WEIGHT may not under any circumstances exceed the permitted limit for the base machine!** See the base machine's user manual to ascertain its limitations and take these into account during operation. The driver is responsible for ensuring that the implement and its load are driven safely.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do **NOT** use the loader / base machine or the implement as a working platform, **NOR** to lift and/or transport people.

- Only qualified individuals are permitted to operate the machine.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.8** — Do NOT use the loader / base machine or the implement as a working platform. (Pictogram: a person standing in/on the raised implement, struck through — prohibited.)

> **NOTE:** **Fig.9** — Do NOT use the loader / base machine or the implement to lift or transport people. (Pictogram: a person being carried in the implement, struck through — prohibited.)

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Always check the machine's surroundings and ensure that all individuals, especially children and animals, have moved away before starting or driving the machine.

Always check the area surrounding the machine. You may not be able to hear warning shouts from people on the outside when you are in the cab with the door closed.

> 🛑 **DANGER:** To prevent serious injury or death from crushing: keep all individuals and objects clear while any part of this machine is in motion.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.10** — Before starting, make sure that no individuals are in the vicinity of the machine. (Pictogram: a loader/base machine with a person standing clear, shown by a double-headed clearance arrow — keep people away from the operating zone.)

Familiarise yourself with the working area and terrain. Pay attention to vertical clearance and limitations that arise due to the increased reach.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.11** — Familiarise yourself with the working area before starting. Maintain a safe distance from electrical cables and other obstructions. (Pictogram: a raised loader near overhead power lines, with a clearance arrow — beware overhead electrical cables and vertical clearance.)

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Always keep your eye on the implement. Objects can fall in front of the base machine or roll backwards onto the operator when the loader arm is raised. Only lift objects that are intended to fit into the implement.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Always look at the implement. Objects can fall out during transport. Only transport loads that are designed for each specific implement.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.12** — Always look at the implement. Objects can fall in front of the base machine or roll backwards onto the driver when the loader is raised. (Pictogram: a raised loader with the load tumbling back toward the cab/operator.)

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Never stand between the front of the base machine and the loader's / base machine's cross tubes.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Risk of crushing and jamming. The loader / base machine may fall down. Lower the loader / base machine / implement to the ground prior to lubrication.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** There are sharp edges. Use personal safety equipment when coming into contact with the implement.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** When installing the hydraulic hoses, check that these are long enough and that they do not get caught between the tool carrier, the loader beam or the links. Adjust the couplings if necessary.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.13** — Never stand between the front of the base machine and the loader's / base machine's cross tubes. (Pictogram: a person standing in the crushing zone between the base machine and the raised loader cross tubes, struck through — prohibited.)

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** **Pressurised oil.** Hydraulic oil at high pressure can be injected into the body in the event of leakage and cause serious injury, blindness or death. Leakage may be invisible. Use approved protective safety glasses and protect the skin using strong leather gloves, for example. Use cardboard or wood for leakage detection. If fluid has entered the skin then it **MUST** be removed within a couple of hours by a doctor who can treat this type of injury.

> **IMPORTANT:** Be very familiar with the LOAD and BASE MACHINE! Carefully read through the base machine's manual and pay attention to its limitations before operation.

> **CAUTION:** If the implement, when coupled to the base machine, is to be transported on public roads: be aware of the applicable traffic provisions within the geographic area where the machinery is being driven, and comply with these without exception.

> **NOTE:** **Fig.14** — NEVER use fingers or hands for leakage detection. (Pictogram: a hand probing a pressurised-fluid jet, struck through — prohibited; decal ref. 0118a.)
