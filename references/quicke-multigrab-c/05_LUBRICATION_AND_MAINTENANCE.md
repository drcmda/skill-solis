# 05 — Lubrication and Maintenance

> **Source:** Quicke Multigrab C / CS Grab Bucket — Instruction Manual, Ålö AB (Quicke), Umeå Sweden — document 60081299 E, 2025-01 (English section of the multilingual manual) — Chapter 05, pp.17–18.

This chapter covers routine lubrication of the attachment's articulation pins, protection of the implement during extended storage, and the inspection, safe leak-detection and repair of the hydraulic hoses and cylinders. The grab bucket is an attachment for a front loader / base machine; cross-references throughout point back to the loader's / base machine's user manual.

## 5.1 Lubrication points

Lubricate the following grease fittings with **universal grease every 10 operating hours**.

> **NOTE:** Use suitable personal protection equipment (PPE).

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Lubricant | Universal grease |
| Interval | Every 10 operating hours |
| PPE | Suitable personal protective equipment required |

**Fig.24 (lubrication-point diagram):** Side view of the Multigrab attachment showing the grease nipples (lubrication nipples) located at each articulation pin. The nipples are present on **both sides** of the attachment, one at each pivot/articulation pin where the grab arms and frame rotate. Lubricate every nipple at each articulation pin on both sides of the attachment.

---

## 5.2 Extended storage

Apply a **thin layer of grease on the visible piston rods** in order to protect them (prevents corrosion of the exposed, polished cylinder rods during long-term storage).

## 5.3 Hydraulic hoses and hydraulic cylinders

> ⚠️ **WARNING — Pressurised hydraulic fluid.**
> Hydraulic fluid at high pressure can be injected into the body in the event of leakage and cause serious injury, blindness or fatality. **Leakage may be invisible.**
> **NEVER use fingers or hands for leakage detection.**
> Use approved protective safety glasses and protect the skin using strong leather gloves, for example. Use cardboard or similar materials for leakage detection. If hydraulic fluid penetrates the skin it can cause serious reactions or infections. **Seek medical attention immediately.** (Fig.25)

**Fig.25 (pictogram):** A red prohibition circle with a diagonal bar over a hand exposed to a fine jet of pressurised fluid from a hose — *NEVER use fingers or hands for leakage detection.*

**Read this before checking the hydraulic system for leakage:**

- Check hydraulic hoses and connections for wear and leakage at regular intervals.
- Ensure that hoses have sufficient clearance and do not scrape against other components.
- Replace damaged hoses and tighten all connections.
- The hydraulic cylinders are **dual-action**. They must always be maintained in good condition for optimal function.
- Leaks, internal or external, affect performance and may be dangerous.
- The implement's hydraulic system operates at high pressure. **Only use spare parts approved by the manufacturer.**

### 5.3.1 Checking the hydraulic connections

Check that all hydraulic hoses and adapters are tightened and that they do not leak.

### 5.3.2 Repairing the hydraulic cylinders

The removal, repair and fitting of the hydraulic cylinders require special tools to prevent damage to internal components. We recommend that a cylinder that needs repairing be sent to an **authorised dealer** to be fixed.
