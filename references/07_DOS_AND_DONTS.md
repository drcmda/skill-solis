# 07 — Do's and Don'ts

> **Source:** Solis 26 Operator Manual (International Tractors Ltd / Sonalika), Publication No. S2618T/J20/28, Rev.1 Aug 2020 — model: Solis 26, Tractor with 9+9 Speed Transmission, Part Code 300190111A — Chapter 07, pp.86–88.

This chapter lists the operating Do's and Don'ts for every major tractor system (engine, fuel, cooling, lubrication, transmission, clutch, hydraulics, brakes, front axle/steering, tyres, electrical) plus fuel- and oil-saving practices and safe-operation guidelines. Following these rules ensures daily care, longer component life, and fewer breakdowns.

## System-by-System DO's

### Engine — DO's

**General**

1. Do ensure the starter key has been released once the engine has started.
2. Do check the proper functioning of the oil pressure gauge and battery charging indicator once the engine has started.
3. Do get the tightness of cylinder head and manifold nuts checked regularly.

**Air Inlet System**

1. Do inspect the air element; clean if necessary.
2. Do check inlet hose and clamps regularly.

**Fuel System**

1. Do drain sediments from the fuel tank periodically.
2. Do clean the fuel tank thoroughly once every 500 hrs.
3. Do change the filter regularly as per the recommended service schedule.
4. Do fill diesel into the tank at the end of the day's work so as to avoid condensation.

**Water Cooling System**

1. Do ensure that the radiator is always filled with clean (soft) water and the radiator cap is tight.
2. Do clean the radiator front grill to ensure free flow of air when the engine is operating.
3. Do ensure proper tension of the fan belt. Deflection should not be more than 10 mm (0.39 inch) when pressure is applied between the fan pulley and the crankshaft pulley.

**Lubrication System**

1. Do replace engine oil after the first 50 hrs of operation; thereafter, engine oil should be replaced every 250 working hrs.
2. Check oil level daily with the tractor parked on level ground.
3. Do replace the lub. oil filter element every 250 working hrs, after the 1st replacement at 50 hrs.
4. Do remove the flywheel housing bottom plug and check for oil traces.

### Transmission — DO's

1. Do change the transmission oil after every 1000 hrs of operation.
2. Do check the condition of the rubber protection bellows on the gear levers periodically, as they prevent infiltration of water and dust into the gear box.

### Clutch — DO's

1. Do ensure that there is clutch free pedal play.
2. Do ensure that the clutch pedal is released slowly while moving the tractor.

### Hydraulic System & Linkage — DO's

1. Do ensure that both hydraulic control levers are in the down position while draining the transmission oil.
2. Do ensure that the hydraulic strainer is cleaned at every schedule.
3. Do adjust the top link for proper length.
4. Do ensure that the lift cover bolts are always tight.
5. Do keep the lower links in the lifted position when the tractor is moving without an implement mounted on it.
6. Do keep the ball joints on the top and lower links clean and dry. **Do not lubricate them.**
7. Do ensure that implements are raised and lowered using the position control lever only and not the draft control lever.

### Braking System — DO's

1. Do keep the brake pedals locked with the interlocking latch when the tractor is not being used in the field.
2. Do use the parking brakes when the vehicle is stationary.
3. Do check loose connections in the linkage mechanism.
4. Do grease the brake pedal bush and brake bracket connections.

### Front Axle & Steering Mechanism — DO's

1. Do lubricate the bushes and steering drag links periodically.
2. Do get the toe-in adjusted by an authorised service centre periodically.
3. Do check the tightness of front and rear wheels to the recommended torque.
4. Do flush oil once a year or 1000 hrs, whichever is earlier, if the tractor is not in operation.

> **NOTE:** Tighten front and rear wheels to the recommended torque (see the torque/specifications chapter for exact values).

### Tyres — DO's

1. Do operate the tractor with correct tyre pressure. This will lead to better traction, longer tyre life and better fuel consumption.

### Electrical — DO's

1. Do ensure that the battery terminals are kept clean.
2. Do ensure the terminal base is lubricated with petroleum jelly.
3. Do earth the tractor by wrapping a chain around the front axle, dropping one end of the chain on the ground.

## System-by-System DON'Ts

### Engine — DON'Ts

**General**

1. Do not keep continuously cranking the engine with the starter key. It will shorten the life of the battery and starter.
2. Do not race the engine in neutral or at the time of cranking.

**Air Inlet System**

1. Do not run the tractor if the air cleaner assembly is defective, as this will lead to impure air being taken in and consequently excessive wear of liners and piston rings.

**Fuel System**

1. Do not keep the fuel tank without a proper sealing cap.
2. Do not use contaminated fuel, as it may affect the operation of the fuel injection pump and the injectors.
3. Do not use bad-quality / spurious filters as replacement.
4. Do not allow leakage through fuel pipe joints.

**Water Cooling System**

1. Do not run the tractor with the radiator cap removed / a non-acting radiator cap.
2. Do not run the tractor when the radiator hoses are leaking, as it will lead to overheating of the engine.
3. Do not remove the thermostat, as it will affect engine performance.
4. Do not run the belt tight, as it will lead to premature failure of the water pump and alternator bearing.
5. Do not run the belt loose, as it will lead to inefficient cooling and improper charging of the battery.

**Lubrication System**

1. Do not use the wrong grade of lubrication oil.
2. Do not mix different brands of engine oil.

**Exhaust System**

1. Do ensure that the exhaust passage is not blocked.

> **NOTE:** The "Exhaust System" item appears under the engine DON'Ts column but is worded as a positive instruction — ensure the exhaust passage is never blocked.

### Transmission — DON'Ts

1. Do not use top gears with low engine rpm.

### Clutch — DON'Ts

1. Do not rest the foot on the clutch pedal.
2. Do not work the tractor by slipping and re-engaging the clutch.
3. Do not coast down steep slopes with the tractor in neutral / with the clutch pedal depressed.

### Hydraulic System & Linkage — DON'Ts

1. Do not move the operational control range to fast response while the tractor is on a hard surface like concrete, as the implement will crash down and get damaged.
2. Do not attempt to pull or tow anything from the top link connection. It is dangerous.
3. Do not use bolts in place of linch pins.
4. Do not reverse the tractor with a PTO-driven implement attached and the PTO lever in the ground PTO position — the implement may get damaged in reverse.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not attempt to pull or tow anything from the top link connection. It is dangerous (risk of tractor rear-over / overturn).

### Braking System — DON'Ts

1. Do not attempt to turn sharply using independent brakes when travelling at high speed. This may cause the tractor to overturn.
2. Do not rest the foot on the brake pedal.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not attempt to turn sharply using independent brakes when travelling at high speed. This may cause the tractor to overturn.

### Front Axle & Steering Mechanism — DON'Ts

1. Do not use the wrong grade of oil for lubrication of the steering gear box.

### Tyres — DON'Ts

1. Do not allow oil, grease and some crop sprays containing considerable amounts of acid and alkalies to contaminate the tyre. These can cause considerable damage to the tyre if they penetrate into the plies through small holes or splits.
2. Do not operate the tractor with excessive tyre pressure.

### Electrical — DON'Ts

1. Do not change the leads of the battery terminals, as this will lead to failure of electrical components.
2. Do not leave the battery leads in the connected position if the tractor is not going to be used for a long period of time.
3. Do not overfill the battery with distilled water. The level should be just enough to submerge the battery plates.
4. Do not do any welding on the tractor without disconnecting the battery terminals.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Always disconnect the battery terminals before any welding on the tractor.

## Save Diesel

- Do switch off the engine when the tractor is not in operation. Avoid unnecessary idling.
- Do operate at optimum speed and in the correct gear.
- Do maintain the recommended tyre pressure for fuel-efficient operation and long life of tyres. Check daily.
- Do use a matching trailer for transportation. Ensure proper hitching. Never overload the trailer.

---

## Every Drop Counts — To Save Oil

- Do not spill fuel or oil while filling or topping up. Use a funnel.
- Do not allow fuel or oil to leak. Ensure that the joints are adequately tight.
- Do not ride the clutch or brake pedal.
- Do not overfill engine oil, as this can cause excessive oil consumption and oil leaks.

## For Better Performance

### Let's Join Hands

- Do maintain your tractor in good working condition.
- Ensure that the transmission is in neutral before starting the engine.
- Fit new sealing rings when the filter elements are changed.
- Keep the air cleaner clean.
- Keep all fuel in clean storage and use a filter when filling the tank.
- Read all operating instructions before commencing to operate the tractor.
- Ensure that safety shields are in place and in good condition.
- Shift into low gear when driving down steep hills.
- Keep the draft control lever fully down when not in use.
- Watch the oil pressure gauge or warning light and investigate any abnormality immediately.
- Allow the engine to cool before removing the radiator filler cap and adding water; remove the radiator cap slowly.
- Attend to minor adjustments and repairs as soon as the necessity is apparent.
- Do replace genuine parts from authorised dealers.
- Latch the brake pedals together when driving on a highway.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Allow the engine to cool before removing the radiator filler cap. Remove the radiator cap slowly to relieve pressure and avoid scalding.

## For Safe Operation

- Do not tamper with the fuel injection pump. (If the seal is broken, the warranty becomes void.)
- Do not use the draft control lever for lifting of implements.
- Do not use the independent brakes for making turns on the highway or at high speeds.
- Do not refuel the tractor with the engine running.
- Do not allow the rear wheel to slip. Use ballast, if necessary.
- Do not operate the tractor or engine while lubricating or cleaning.
- Do not use inferior-quality lubricants; use only the recommended grade.
- Do not start the tractor in an enclosed building unless the doors and windows are open for proper ventilation.
- Do not run the engine with the air cleaner disconnected.
- Do not allow the engine to idle for a long period.
- Do not use worn-out tyres.
- Do not start the engine with the PTO engaged.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not start the tractor/engine in an enclosed building unless doors and windows are open for proper ventilation (exhaust fumes / carbon monoxide hazard).

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not refuel the tractor with the engine running (fire hazard).

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not operate the tractor or engine while lubricating or cleaning (entanglement / crush hazard).

> **NOTE (closing instruction, p.88):** Ensure daily care of your tractor to avoid breakdowns. Carefully read and follow the other instructions given in the DO's and DON'Ts maintenance booklet, to ensure maximum saving of oil.
