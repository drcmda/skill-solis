The instrument cluster diagram carries a large central tachometer/hour-meter flanked left by the temperature gauge and right by the fuel gauge, surrounded by 14 telltale indicator icons (callouts 1–14). The table below maps each icon to its indicator; detailed descriptions follow in 3.2.1–3.2.14.

| No. | Icon (described) | Indicator |
|-----|------------------|-----------|
| 1 | Parking brake symbol — "(P)" / circle with "!" inside, in brackets | Parking Brake Indicator |
| 2 | Battery symbol (rectangular cell with +/− terminals) | Battery Charging Indicator |
| 3 | Air cleaner / filter element symbol | Air Cleaner Clogging Indicator |
| 4 | Left-pointing arrow (turn signal) | Left Turn Indicator |
| 5 | Tachometer dial with needle | RPM & Hour Meter |
| 6 | Right-pointing arrow (turn signal) | Right Turn Indicator |
| 7 | Headlamp beam with horizontal rays (high beam) | High Beam Indicator |
| 8 | Coiled glow-plug / heater-element symbol | Glow Plug Indicator |
| 9 | Oil-can / oil-pressure symbol | Oil Pressure Indicator |
| 10 | PTO shaft symbol (gear/circle with rays) | PTO Monitor Lamp |
| 11 | Trailer with turn-signal arrows symbol | Turn Trailer Indicator |
| 12 | Thermometer in liquid (coolant temperature) | Temperature Gauge |

| 14 | Work lamp / plough lamp symbol (lamp with downward rays) | Working Lamp Indicator |

### 3.2.1 Park Brake Indicator

It glows when the parking brake is engaged.

### 3.2.2 Battery Charge Indicator

This indicator indicates whether the battery is being charged or not. Refer to the conditions table below for the meaning under different states:

| Ignition Switch | Engine | Indicator | Battery Charging System Functioning |
|-----------------|--------|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| ON | OFF | GLOW | OK |
| ON | OFF | OFF | Charging System / Battery is defective, Get both thing checked from electrician |
| ON | Start/Running | OFF | Battery being Charged |
| ON | Start/Running | GLOW | Charging System is defective / Battery is draining out, get the charging system checked from electrician |

### 3.2.3 Air Cleaner Clogging Indicator

This light glows when the air filter gets choked. Clean the air cleaner element immediately with air pressure if this light is glowing.

### 3.2.4 Left Turn Indicator

It glows when the left side indicator is switched ON.

### 3.2.5 Engine RPM cum Hour Meter

The needle of this meter indicates the speed of the engine in revolutions per minute, and the hour meter indicates the number of hours worked by the engine. The dial face is marked with a **green zone** (safe operating zone).

Green Zone is safe for operation.

> **NOTE:** Hour meter may differ from actual hours (as per clock); this is purely dependent on Engine RPM.

### 3.2.6 Right Turn Indicator

It glows when the right side indicator is switched ON.

### 3.2.7 High Beam Indicator

This light glows when the head lights are in high beam mode.

### 3.2.8 Cold Start Indication

It glows when the ignition heater (glow plug) is ON at the second position of the starting key.

### 3.2.9 Oil Pressure Indicator

This light glows when engine oil pressure is low.

### 3.2.10 PTO Monitor Lamp

This lamp will turn ON when the PTO is engaged.

### 3.2.11 Turn Trailer Lamp

This light glows when a trailer lamp load is attached and the turn indicator switch is ON. It also glows all the time when the hazard switch is ON.

### 3.2.12 Temperature Gauge

This gauge indicates the temperature of engine coolant. The **GREEN** zone indicates normal temperature and the **RED** zone indicates engine overheating. If the needle moves beyond the normal range, towards the RED zone, follow the procedure:

1. Drive safely to the side of the road and park your tractor.
2. Allow the engine to run idle.
3. If the temperature does not go down, shut off the engine and allow sufficient time for it to cool.
4. Visually inspect the fan belt for looseness, breakage and all water hose connections for leaks.
5. If the fan belt is OK and no coolant leak is noticed, check the coolant level.
6. Add coolant if required, otherwise contact your nearest dealer.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not remove the radiator cap when the engine and radiator are hot. Hot coolant and steam may blow out under pressure, which could cause serious injury. The cap should only be taken off when the coolant temperature has lowered. Necessary precaution to be taken while opening the radiator cap.

### 3.2.13 Fuel Gauge

The fuel gauge gives an approximate indication of the quantity of fuel in the fuel tank. The dial is marked with **Red**, **Amber**, and **Green** zones. If the needle enters the RED zone, refill the fuel tank.

> **NOTE:** Ensure a minimum of 6 Litre [1.32 U.S. Gallon] of fuel in the fuel tank to avoid air locking.

### 3.2.14 Work Lamp Indicator

This light glows when the work lamp or plough lamp is ON.

## 3.3 Dashboard Controls

The dashboard layout (Fig. 9.6) shows, around the steering wheel: the **Instrument Cluster** (top right of column), **Beacon (Revolving) Light Switch** and **Hazard Warning Light Switch** (left of column), the **Combination Switch** and **Ignition Switch** (right side of column), and the **PTO External Control Switch** (right side).

### 3.3.1 Hazard Warning Light Switch (Fig. 3.3.1)

Push this switch to blink all indicators in a HAZARD situation to alert others.

Purpose of the hazard switch is as follows:

1. All the four lights blinking indicates that the driver has no control on the tractor.
2. Mechanical defects in the tractor.

### 3.3.2 Combination Switch (Fig. 3.3.2)

The combination switch (steering-column stalk, Fig. 3.3.2) integrates the turn-signal switch (A), the horn (B), and the head-light/parking-light rotary switch (C).

**Side Indicator Switch (A):**
This switch is used for indicating the vehicle turn. Move the turn signal lever left to indicate a left (L) hand turn or right (R) for a right hand turn. Indicator lights will flash accordingly.

**Horn Switch (B):**
Press this switch to blow the horn.

**Head Light and Parking Light Switch (C):**
This switch illuminates all lights (Parking Light, Head Light, High Beam, Low Beam) with the clockwise rotation.

Rotary position detail:

| Position | Action | Lights ON |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| OFF Position | — | All lights are off |
| 1st Position (Clockwise) | With 1st click stop | Parking lights, instrument panel lights and tail lights glow |
| 2nd Position (Clockwise) | With 2nd click stop | Head lights (Low beam), instrument panel lights, parking lights and tail lights glow |
| 3rd Position (Clockwise) | With 3rd click stop | Head lights (High beam), instrument panel lights, parking lights and tail lights glow |
| Horn | Press the combination switch | Blows the horn |

### 3.3.3 Starting Key (Ignition) Switch

Functioning of the starting key switch is as below:

| Position | Name | Function |
|----------|------|----------|
| 1st Position | OFF | All the electrical systems remain disconnected in this position. |
| 2nd Position | ON & HEATER | The warning lights (Battery, Oil Pressure indicator) will be functional in this position. This is the normal running position after the engine is started. Glow plug indication in the instrument cluster will glow at this position. |
| 3rd Position | START | Immediately after the use of the air heater, turn the key further clockwise to the Start position to start the engine. |

Key rotation order (clockwise): OFF → ON+HEATER → START → returns to ON.

> ⚠️ **NOTE / CAUTION:**
> - Do not keep the starter engaged more than 5–8 seconds. If the engine stalls/fails to start then wait for 5–10 seconds before re-engaging the starter, otherwise you may damage it.
> - Keep the switch in OFF condition when the engine is also in OFF condition.

### 3.3.4 Revolving Light Switch (Fig. 3.3.4)

This switch is used to turn ON/OFF the revolving light mounted on the ROPS.

### 3.3.5 PTO External Control Switch (Fig. 3.3.5)

When this switch is ON, the operator can get up from the driver seat (after the park brake is applied) to use the stationary PTO application.

## 3.5 Battery

The battery is located at the front side of the tractor on the front axle bracket. Open the bonnet to access the battery (see Fig. 3.5a).

**Battery Cut-off Switch (Fig. 3.5b):**
A battery cut-off switch is provided to connect or disconnect power supply from the battery. When the tractor is not in use for a longer period, rotate the switch anti-clockwise to switch off the power supply. The switch is marked with **OFF POSITION** and **ON POSITION** (Fig. 3.5b).

## 3.9 Driver's Seat

While seating, adjust the weight of the operator with the weight adjustment knob provided at the back side of the seat so as to be comfortable for driving and to minimize vibrations. With the front & rear adjusting knob, slide the seat so as to have a comfortable approach towards all levers. The effort range that can be adjusted while sitting on the seat is **50–120 kgf [110.2 – 264.5 lbf]**.

**Horizontal Adjustment**
- Lift the lever (1, Fig. 3.9a) to move the seat forward and backward.

**Vertical Adjustment**
- Use knob (2, Fig. 3.9b) to adjust the suspension.
- Use knob (3, Fig. 3.9b) to adjust the height of the seat vertically.
- Seat belt (4, Fig. 3.9c) for safety.

**Adjustment of Arm Rest Inclination:**
- Raise the arm rest in the upward position.
- Rotate knob (5, Fig. 3.9d) in the clockwise direction to increase the arm rest inclination.
- Rotate knob (5, Fig. 3.9d) in the anti-clockwise direction to decrease the arm rest inclination.

> ⚠️ **CAUTION — To avoid personal injury:**
> - Make adjustments to the seat only while the tractor is stopped.
> - Make sure that the seat is completely secured after each adjustment.
> - Do not allow any person other than the operator to ride on the tractor.
> - Always use the seat belt when the ROPS is installed.
> - Do not use the seat belt if the tractor is not equipped with ROPS.

| 1 | Horizontal (fore/aft) slide lever | Fig. 3.9a |
| 2 | Suspension adjustment knob | Fig. 3.9b |
| 3 | Seat height (vertical) adjustment knob | Fig. 3.9b |
