## §11 — Technical Servicing (Technische Bedienung)

The front loader (Frontlader) is a durable, reliable machine that requires no adjustments. Long-term, efficient operation of the loader depends on proper operation, adequate lubrication, immediate correction of any defects found, and proper maintenance.

**Daily — after work is finished:**

- Thoroughly clean the loader and inspect its condition. Correct any faults found.
- Pay particular attention to the tightness of the bolts and nuts that secure the subframe/substructure (Unterkonstruktion). If there is any play (slack) in a connection, retighten it. For the tightening torques of bolted connections, see Section 6.2.
- Inspect the hydraulic system for leaks and carry out the required lubrication of the loader.
- Make sure the hydraulic couplings (Hydraulikkupplungen) are clean. Cleanliness of the hydraulic connections is necessary to prevent mechanical contamination of the oil — and thereby of the precision components of the hydraulic system.
- Regularly eliminate all leaks in the hydraulic system by tightening the fasteners and by replacing the sealing rings and any damaged system components.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** Because of natural material wear, hydraulic hoses (Hydraulikschläuche) should be replaced periodically every 4 years from their date of manufacture. The manufacturing date is marked on the hydraulic hoses as the year and the week number.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** If you have difficulties operating your loader, please contact the manufacturer, who will advise you or send you a service technician.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** All maintenance work may only be carried out with the bucket (grab) (Schaufel / Greifer) lowered to the ground and the tractor engine switched off, or with the loader at a standstill.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** The environment can be endangered by oil leaks from the hydraulic system. All work on the hydraulic system should be carried out so that no oil can penetrate into the ground.

## §12 — Lubrication (Schmierung)

The loader can be lubricated when it is in its rest position (Ruhestellung), or when it is mounted on the tractor and supported on the ground. The list of lubrication points is shown in Fig. 12.

> **NOTE:** *Fig. 12 — Lubrication diagram (Schmierdiagramm).* The diagram shows the loader in side profile with the grease points marked along the pivoting linkage. Grease points are located at the articulated/pivot joints of the loader: the subframe/console pivots, the lift arm (boom / Ausleger) pivot bearings, the lift-cylinder (Hubzylinder) pin joints, the tilt-cylinder (Kippzylinder) pin joints, and the tool carrier / quick-attach frame (Schnellwechselrahmen) pivot. Each marked point is a ball-type grease nipple (Kugelschmierer / Schmiernippel).

**Grease / lubricant to use:**

| Item | Specification |
|---|---|
| Lubricant for the front loader | **ŁT-42** or **ŁT-43** grease |
| Grease-point type | Ball-type grease nipples (Kugelschmierer) |
| Application tool | Manual piston grease gun (Kolbenschmierpresse) |

**Procedure:**

- The front loader has lubrication points in the form of ball-type grease nipples. Fill the grease nipples using a manual piston grease gun.
- Clean each grease nipple before fitting (applying) the grease gun.

**Lubrication interval:**

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| Every **25 operating hours** of the loader | Re-grease all lubrication points |
| After **every interruption in operation longer than one month** | Re-grease all lubrication points |

## §13 — Storage (Lagerung)

At the end of the working season, you should do the following:

- Carefully clean the loader of dirt.
- Carry out a technical inspection.
- Lubricate the loader in accordance with §12.
- Surfaces from which the paint layer has been removed due to friction or collision must be protected against rust (primed / grundiert).
- Coat the unpainted areas with a dense layer of grease.
- When storing the socket and plug of the hydraulic system, secure the plugs and sockets with caps/connectors so that no dirt or moisture can enter the system.

**Storage location and position:**

- Store the loader in a dry room, protected from precipitation, on a horizontal, level and hardened (cured) surface.
- Store the loader in its rest position (Ruheposition, Fig. 4), supported on the working part (attachment) and on its stands/supports (Stützen).
- The loader must be secured against access by unauthorized persons, in particular children.

## §14 — Preparing the Front Loader Before the Season (Vorbereitung vor Saisonbeginn)

Before the start of the season, or after a prolonged standstill of the loader, do the following:

- Check that all bolts and nuts are firmly seated.
- Check all bolted connections.
- Check the condition of the hydraulic hoses (Hydraulikschläuche) and quick couplings (Schnellkupplungen).
- Replace damaged parts with new ones.
- Check the technical condition of the tractor, which should be equipped with an efficient (properly functioning) hydraulic system.
- Lubricate all points in accordance with Section 12 "Lubrication".
- Check the correct functioning of the hydraulic system — i.e. raise the boom (Ausleger) and rotate/tilt the bucket (Schaufel).
- Check the loader's hydraulic system for leaks.
- Complete or replace any illegible warning and information decals (Warn- und Informationsaufkleber).

## §15 — Disassembly and Disposal (Demontage und Entsorgung)

For environmental-protection reasons, when the machine is completely worn out it should be handled as follows [the German source sentence reads "…sollte die Maschine … verwendet werden" (lit.: "should be *used*"), which is a defect in the original — context (drain oil, knock out pins, disassemble, scrap) makes clear it means disposed of / dismantled]:

- Thoroughly drain the oil from the hydraulic system.
- Knock out the safety pins and split pins (Sicherheitsnadeln und -stifte).
- Disassemble the front loader.
- Take the metal parts of the machine to the scrapyard.
- Worn flexible hydraulic hoses should be handed over to the businesses/facilities that deal with this problem (i.e. specialist disposal).

Please be aware of the possibility of contaminating the environment with hydraulic oil. Collect the oil in a sealed container and hand it over to a disposal point.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** Be especially careful during disassembly, and use the drawings in the parts catalogue (Teilekatalog)!
