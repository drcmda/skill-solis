## 10.1 Preparing the Front Loader for Operation

(VORBEREITUNG DES FRONTLADERS AUF DEN BETRIEB)

Before operating the front loader (Frontlader), carry out the following:

- Check the completeness of all components/assemblies (Bauteile).
- Check that all bolts and nuts are firmly seated; pay particular attention to the bolts that connect the support/load-bearing structure (Tragkonstruktion) to the tractor.
- Check the condition of the connecting cables/hoses and the quick-couplers (Schnellkupplungen).
- Check the unit for oil leaks (Ölleckagen).
- Replace any damaged parts with new ones.
- Lubricate the loader as described in Section 12 of this manual.
- Check the technical condition of the tractor: the tractor should be fitted with an efficient/serviceable hydraulic system; check the oil level in the tractor's housing/case (Traktorkasten).
- Connect the front loader to the tractor as described in Section 7.
- Verify the function of all hydraulic-system components by attempting to operate the control levers of the control valve/distributor (Steuerhebel des Verteilers).

## 10.2 Transport Travel

(TRANSPORTFAHRTEN)

When driving on public roads, be especially careful and observe the applicable traffic regulations. Road travel may only take place **after the work implement (Arbeitsgerät) has been removed**.

Procedure for preparing the loader for transport:

- Raise the boom/arm (Ausleger) so that the pivot point of the quick-attach frame (Schnellspannrahmen / Schnellwechselrahmen) is approximately **0.5 m** high.
- Fit mechanical locks (mechanische Schlösser) on the cylinders that raise the boom.
- Attach the storage/parking supports (Lagerungsstützen) to the counterweight (Gegengewicht).
- Fit a triangular slow-moving-vehicle marker (dreieckiges Unterscheidungszeichen) at the rear of the tractor.
- Adapt the travel speed to the type and condition of the road.

**Recommended travel speeds:**

| Road / surface | Maximum speed |
|---|---|
| Roads with a smooth surface | up to 15 km/h |
| Field roads or unpaved/gravel roads | 6 – 10 km/h |
| Bumpy / rough roads | up to 5 km/h |

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** When driving on public roads, be especially careful and comply with the applicable road-traffic regulations (Straßenverkehrsordnung).

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** Driving on public roads with the implement attached (mit angebautem Gerät) is prohibited.

## 10.3 Operating the Front Loader

(BETRIEB DES FRONTLADERS)

When using the loader, follow these steps:

- Drive up to the pile/heap (Halde) and slowly drive the working unit (Arbeitseinheit) into the material to be loaded.
- Raise the boom/arm (Ausleger) and back the tractor up.
- Drive to the transport vehicle (e.g. trailer) and unload the material.
- Back the tractor up and lower the boom.

When operating the loader, position the transport vehicle (e.g. trailer / Anhänger) as favourably as possible relative to the pile. Choose the distance so that the unit is manoeuvred over the shortest possible path.

The front loader is designed for **loading and unloading**. In rare cases it may be used as a means of transport over short distances. In that case:

- Limit the travel speed to **6 km/h**.
- Lower the bucket (Schaufel) so that the working element does **not** restrict the driver's view.

The speed limit is dictated by the need to reduce dynamic loads (dynamische Belastungen) that could damage the tractor.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** The presence of persons on the loader during work and transport is **unacceptable**, as it can lead to permanent disability or even death. No people or animals may be in the vicinity of the working loader, due to the danger of being run over. Pay particular attention to children.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** The loader may only be operated by trained persons who are authorised to drive the tractor. Children must be excluded from the work and from handling the equipment.

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE:** The front loader must not be operated on slopes steeper than **12° along the slope (uphill/downhill)** or **8° across the slope (sideways)**, because of the danger that the tractor and front loader will tip over. The risk of tipping is increased when the bucket is raised with the maximum load.

> **NOTE:** Read the load/lift-height table below before starting work. This table is supplied with the operating manual — place it in a clearly visible position while operating the loader.

## Load Capacity vs. Lift Height (Stability Table & Diagram)

The manual gives the maximum lift height and the rated lifting capacity at the implement pivot point (Werkzeug-Drehpunkt), measured at the bottom and at the top of the lift range. Lifting capacity decreases as lift height increases.

**Model Ł-103U [300 kg]**

| Designation (Bezeichnung) | Symbol | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum lift height (Maximale Hubhöhe) | H max | **2220 mm** |
| Lifting capacity at the tool pivot point, low/bottom (Hubkraft am Werkzeug-Drehpunkt unten) | Q1 | **500 kg** |
| Lifting capacity at the tool pivot point, high/top (Hubkraft am Werkzeug-Drehpunkt oben) | Q2 | **300 kg** |

**Diagram — Lift height H [mm] (vertical axis, "Hubhöhe H [mm]") vs. load capacity Q [kg] (horizontal axis, "Traglast Q [kg]"):** a downward-sloping curve plotting lift height against load. The vertical axis is marked at **H max** (top, = 2220 mm) and **½ H max** (mid-height); the horizontal axis is marked **Q2** (left) and **Q1** (right). At the lowest position the loader can lift up to **Q1 = 500 kg**; as the boom is raised to **H max = 2220 mm**, the safe capacity falls to **Q2 = 300 kg** (the rated 300 kg class capacity). Do not exceed these limits.

> ⚠️ **WARNING:** Do not exceed the rated lifting capacities (Q1 = 500 kg low / Q2 = 300 kg at full height). Overloading — and especially raising a maximum load to full height — sharply increases the risk of the tractor and loader tipping over.
